# AQLIYA Knowledge Foundation Charter v1.0

**Status:** FROZEN  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Effective date:** 2026-06-09  
**Amendment policy:** Changes require a formal ADR or a new version release (v1.1+). Do not modify in place during active implementation.

**Governing statement:**

> **This Foundation shall always privilege authoritative professional judgment over automated inference.**

**Operating philosophy:**

```text
Knowledge First
Human Governed
AI Assisted
```

---

## 1. Purpose

AQLIYA Knowledge Foundation exists to provide a governed, traceable, versioned, and professionally defensible knowledge infrastructure for all AQLIYA intelligence systems.

The Foundation is not a document repository.  
The Foundation is a governed knowledge operating system.

Its purpose is to transform authoritative professional knowledge into executable intelligence while preserving governance, traceability, and human accountability.

---

## 2. Core Principles

### 2.1 Knowledge Before AI

Knowledge is the primary asset. AI is an execution layer.

Required sequence:

```text
Authority
↓
Governance
↓
Classification
↓
Knowledge
↓
Lineage
↓
Memory
↓
AI
```

No intelligence feature may bypass this sequence.

### 2.2 No Orphan Knowledge

No knowledge asset may enter AQLIYA unless it contains:

- Authority Source
- Version Information
- Confidence Information
- Knowledge Lineage

Assets that do not meet these requirements remain external references and are not admitted into the Foundation.

### 2.3 Human-Defensible Intelligence

Every recommendation, classification, mapping, disclosure suggestion, audit finding, local content conclusion, or generated output must be explainable.

Required explanation chain:

```text
Decision
↓
Rule
↓
Standard
↓
Source
↓
Reviewer
```

No output may rely solely on model-generated reasoning.

## 3. Authority Model

### 3.1 Authority Level A — Primary Authorities

- IFRS Foundation
- IAASB
- SOCPA
- LCGPA
- Official Government Regulators

These authorities may create executable rules.

### 3.2 Authority Level B — Professional Authorities

- IFAC
- ACCA
- ICAEW
- AICPA
- CPA Canada

These authorities may create implementation guidance.

### 3.3 Authority Level C — Professional Publications

- Big Four Publications
- Major Professional Bodies

These authorities may create interpretation support.

### 3.4 Authority Level D — Academic Sources

- Universities
- Research Institutions
- Peer Reviewed Research

These authorities may support research and validation.

### 3.5 Authority Level E — Industry Commentary

- Industry Analysis
- Commentary
- Opinion Content

These authorities may not create executable rules.

---

## 4. Asset Classification Model

### 4.1 Type A — Authority Rules

Examples: IFRS, ISA, ISQM, SOCPA, LCGPA  
Purpose: Executable governance and rules.

### 4.2 Type B — Implementation Guidance

Examples: Guides, Educational Material, Methodologies  
Purpose: Implementation assistance.

### 4.3 Type C — Structured Objects

Examples: Chart of Accounts, ERP Structures, Mapping Libraries  
Purpose: Machine-readable operational structures.

### 4.4 Type D — Operational Intelligence

Examples: Risk Libraries, Findings Libraries, Procedures, Evidence Catalogs  
Purpose: Operational execution.

### 4.5 Type E — Institutional Memory

Examples: Firm Decisions, Reviewer Feedback, Classification History, Approval History  
Purpose: Firm-specific learning.

---

## 5. Confidence Model

Every asset must contain:

- Authority Level
- Validation Status
- Version Status
- Review Status
- Confidence Score

Indicative confidence ranges:

- Authority A + Validated + Current Version = 95–100
- Authority B/C Interpretation = 70–90
- Firm Memory + Repeated Approval = 85–99
- Unvalidated Assets = Below 70

---

## 6. Version Governance

Every governed asset must contain:

- Asset ID
- Version Label
- Issue Date
- Effective Date
- Superseded Date
- Jurisdiction
- Status
- Source URL
- Source Owner

Version history must never be destroyed.

---

## 7. Lineage Requirements

Every decision must be traceable.

Minimum lineage chain:

```text
Decision
↓
Rule
↓
Authority
↓
Source Document
↓
Reviewer
```

Every downstream output must maintain linkage to upstream knowledge assets.

---

## 8. Ontology Requirements

All assets must participate in a governed ontology.

Minimum ontology fields:

- Domain
- Subdomain
- Entity
- Relationship
- Authority
- Usage

Ontology relationships become the foundation for:

- TB Intelligence
- Mapping Engine
- Disclosure Engine
- Audit Intelligence
- Local Content Intelligence

---

## 9. Non-Negotiable Constraints

1. **No Autonomous Professional Decisions**  
   AI may recommend. Humans approve.

2. **No Unattributed Knowledge**  
   Every governed asset must have source attribution.

3. **No Versionless Knowledge**  
   Every governed asset must belong to a version policy.

4. **No Untraceable Conclusions**  
   Every conclusion must maintain lineage.

5. **No Direct Rule Creation By LLMs**  
   LLMs may suggest candidate rules. Humans approve executable rules.
