## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document is the result of a comprehensive deep research analysis of the Saudi Local Content and Government Procurement Authority (LCGPA) regulatory ecosystem. Over 50+ sources were analyzed including official government publications, press releases, auditor guides, consultant analyses, and industry frameworks.

### Key Findings

1. **LCGPA is the national authority** for local content policy, established 2018 under Council of Ministers Resolution No. 551
2. **Local Content is defined as** total spending in KSA on Saudi components within labor, goods, services, assets, and technology (value added to GDP)
3. **The measurement formula** breaks into 4 components: Workforce Compensation, Goods & Services, Asset Depreciation, Capacity Building
4. **Six mechanisms** drive compliance: LC Score in Commercial Evaluation, Minimum LC Threshold, Price Preference (10%), Mandatory List, Localization of Industry & Knowledge Transfer, Economic Participation
5. **Certificate validity:** 19 months from end of financial year
6. **Mandatory List:** 400+ products across 11+ sectors, expanding continuously
7. **Minimum LC requirements** being phased in from Aug 2026 through 2031 (233+ products initially)
8. **Cabinet Resolution 658 (2024)** extends requirements to state-owned entities (>50% government ownership)
9. **AUP (Agreed Upon Procedures)** reports required from licensed auditors
10. **Separation from IKTVA:** LCGPA is national; IKTVA is Saudi Aramco-specific

## 3. PHASE 2: LOCAL CONTENT FRAMEWORK

### 3.1 Official Definition

> **"Local content is the total spending in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia on Saudi components within labor, goods, services, assets, and technology. In other words, it is the value added to the GDP as a result of spending on national elements — i.e., human resources, products, services and technology."**

— LCGPA Official Definition
Source: https://www.watermeetsmoney.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/Saudi-roadshow-3-LCGPA.pdf

Arabic definition (from LCGPA website):
> المحتوى المحلي هو إجمالي الإنفاق في المملكة العربية السعودية على المكونات السعودية ضمن العمالة والسلع والخدمات والأصول والتقنية.

### 3.2 LCGPA Vision, Mission & Values

**Vision:**
> "Nurturing local potential and capitalizing the national purchasing power for a strong and sustainable economy."

**Mission:**
> "Developing and monitoring knowledge-based policies and tools, enhance awareness and participation in the development of local content among all targeted segments."

**Values:**
1. **Innovation** — Generating new ideas and models
2. **Collaboration** — Integration with various entities and expertise exchange
3. **Empowerment** — Launching enabling policies and tools
4. **Delivering Impact** — Maximizing pride and authenticity in local content
5. **Sustainability** — Continuous development of local capabilities

### 3.3 Strategic Goals (Saudi Vision 2030 Alignment)

LCGPA contributes to TWO pillars of Saudi Vision 2030:
- **Thriving Economy**
- **Ambitious Nation**

**Economic Impact Objectives:**
1. Development of local companies by directing government demand to their products and services
2. Empowering human resources
3. Creating attractive environment for investments in KSA
4. Identifying government demand and localization opportunities
5. Localization of industry and knowledge transfer

### 3.4 Core Principles

1. **GDP Value Capture** — LC = value added to Saudi GDP
2. **Spend-Based Measurement** — LC calculated from actual expenditures
3. **Entity-Level Baseline** — Historical performance drives current score
4. **Continuous Improvement** — Target scores must increase over contract period
5. **Verified Data** — All data must be auditable via AUP reports
6. **Economic Multiplier Effect** — Local spend creates downstream economic benefits
7. **Transparency** — Standardized measurement methodology
8. **Accountability** — Contractual obligations for LC commitments

### 3.5 Local Content Elements (5 Components)

```
LOCAL CONTENT = Σ (
    Workforce Compensation (Labor)
    + Goods & Services (Supply Chain)
    + Asset Depreciation (Capital)
    + Capacity Building (R&D/Training)
    + Technology (Innovation)
)
```

### 3.6 Six Local Content Mechanisms

| Level | Mechanism | Description |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| **Project** | 1. LC Score in Commercial Evaluation | Weighted LC score in bid evaluation (e.g., 40% of commercial) |
| **Project** | 2. Minimum LC Threshold | Minimum % required to be eligible to bid |
| **Product** | 3. Price Preference (10%) | National products get 10% price advantage |
| **Product** | 4. Mandatory List | Products that MUST be sourced locally |
| **Investment** | 5. LIKT (Localization of Industry & Knowledge Transfer) | Off-take agreements for localization |
| **Investment** | 6. Economic Participation | Contribution via contracts >100M SAR imported goods |

Source: LCGPA Official Presentation (Water Meets Money Roadshow)

## 4. PHASE 3: LOCAL CONTENT MEASUREMENT

### 4.1 The Local Content Formula

```
Local Content Score (%) = 
    (LC in Labor Compensation + LC in Goods & Services + LC in Asset Depreciation + LC in Capacity Building)
    ÷
    (Total Cost of Labor Compensation + Total Cost of Goods & Services + Total Cost of Asset Depreciation + Total Cost of Capacity Building)
    × 100
```

### 4.2 Formula Breakdown

#### Numerator — Local Content Components

| Component | What Counts as Local | Calculation |
|-----------|---------------------|-------------|
| Labor Compensation | Saudi employees: 100% of salary; Non-Saudi: 37% of salary | Σ(Saudi salaries × 100%) + Σ(Non-Saudi salaries × 37%) |
| Goods & Services | Spend on locally sourced/produced goods & services | Σ(Local supplier spend × 100%) + Σ(Imported supplier spend × LC factor) |
| Asset Depreciation | Assets manufactured in KSA: 100% depreciation; Imported: 20% | Σ(Local asset depreciation × 100%) + Σ(Imported asset depreciation × 20%) |
| Capacity Building | R&D spending + Employee training + Supplier development | Σ(Eligible CB expenditures × 100%) |

#### Denominator — Total Costs

| Component | What's Included |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Labor Compensation | Total payroll (all employees, Saudi + non-Saudi) |
| Goods & Services | Total procurement spend (local + imported) |
| Asset Depreciation | Total depreciation expense for all assets |
| Capacity Building | Total CB spending |

### 4.3 Required Datasets

1. **Financial Statements** — Audited P&L, balance sheet, expense breakdown
2. **Payroll Register** — Employee list by nationality, salary, position
3. **Procurement Ledger** — Supplier list by local/foreign, spend amounts, product categories
4. **Fixed Asset Register** — Asset list by origin (local/imported), depreciation schedules
5. **Capacity Building Records** — Training records, R&D project reports, supplier development programs
6. **Supplier Information** — Ownership structure, local content declarations, SASO certifications
7. **Manufacturer Declarations** — Product origin certifications, manufacturing location proof

### 4.4 Calculation Methodology (Step-by-Step)

**Step 1: Data Collection**
- Extract financial data for the fiscal year
- Prepare payroll, procurement, asset, and CB datasets
- Classify all expenditures by local vs. foreign origin

**Step 2: Classification Rules**
- **Company Ownership:** ≥50% Saudi-owned = local supplier
- **Manufacturing Origin:** Product manufactured in KSA = local product
- **Workforce:** Saudi nationality = 100% LC; Expatriate = 37% LC
- **Assets:** Made in KSA = 100% LC; Imported = 20% LC

**Step 3: Component Calculation**
- Calculate each component using the formula above
- Sum all LC contributions (numerator)
- Sum all total costs (denominator)
- Divide and multiply by 100

**Step 4: Validation**
- Cross-reference with audited financials
- Verify supplier declarations
- Check manufacturer certifications
- Confirm asset origin documentation

**Step 5: AUP Audit**
- Licensed auditor performs Agreed Upon Procedures
- Auditor verifies data accuracy and methodology compliance
- AUP report submitted to LCGPA portal

**Step 6: Certificate Issuance**
- LCGPA reviews AUP report
- Local Content Certificate issued
- Valid for 19 months from end of fiscal year

## 5. PHASE 4: MEASUREMENT COMPONENTS

### 5.1 LABOR (Workforce Compensation)

#### Required Data
1. Employee name
2. Nationality (Saudi/Non-Saudi)
3. Position/Job title
4. Base salary
5. Allowances (housing, transportation, etc.)
6. Bonuses and commissions
7. Total annual compensation
8. Employment contract
9. Social insurance (GOSI) registration
10. Work permit (Iqama) for expatriates

#### Classification Rules
| Employee Type | LC Factor | Evidence Required |
|--------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Saudi National (full-time) | 100% of salary | GOSI registration, Saudi ID, contract |
| Saudi National (part-time) | 100% of salary | Contract, GOSI, attendance records |
| Non-Saudi (full-time) | 37% of salary | Iqama, contract, work permit |
| Non-Saudi (part-time) | 37% of salary | Contract, Iqama, attendance |
| Board Members (Saudi) | 100% of compensation | Board resolution, commercial registration |
| Board Members (Non-Saudi) | 37% of compensation | Board resolution, Iqama if resident |

#### Exceptions & Special Cases
- **Seconded employees:** Treated as per secondment agreement
- **Contractors/consultants:** Classified as services, not labor
- **Seasonal workers:** Pro-rated based on months worked
- **Family members:** Paid family members treated as employees
- **GOSI registration:** Required for Saudi employees to qualify

#### Supporting Evidence
- GOSI subscription certificates
- Saudi ID copies (for Saudi employees)
- Iqama copies (for non-Saudi employees)
- Employment contracts
- Payroll registers (signed)
- Bank transfer records
- Annual leave records
- Attendance records

### 5.2 GOODS & SERVICES

#### Supplier Classifications

| Classification | Criteria | LC Treatment |
|---------------|----------|--------------|
| Local Manufacturer | ≥50% Saudi-owned, factory in KSA, manufacturing license | 100% of spend counted as local |
| Local Distributor | ≥50% Saudi-owned, but distributes imported goods | Only distribution margin counts as local |
| Importer/Distributor | <50% Saudi-owned or no local operations | Only in-KSA costs count |
| Foreign Supplier | No KSA presence | 0% local content (only KSA-registered expenses) |
| Service Provider (Local) | ≥50% Saudi-owned, KSA operations | 100% of service fee |
| Service Provider (Foreign) | <50% Saudi-owned | Only KSA-based costs |

#### Manufacturer Rules
1. Must have valid industrial license from Ministry of Industry
2. Must have factory location within KSA
3. Manufacturing process must be substantial (not just assembly/packaging)
4. Must provide manufacturer declaration form
5. At least 50% Saudi ownership for full LC recognition
6. Compliance with SASO standards and specifications

#### Distributor Rules
1. Only distribution margin (not total product cost) counts as local
2. Must have ≥50% Saudi ownership
3. Must have commercial registration and distribution license
4. Imported products sold by local distributor → only margin is local
5. Must provide supplier declaration and origin documentation

#### Required Evidence
- Commercial registration (CR)
- Industrial license (for manufacturers)
- SASO certificate of conformity
- Manufacturer/Supplier declaration form
- Purchase orders and contracts
- Invoices (local and foreign)
- Proof of payment (bank transfers)
- Import customs declarations (for imported goods)
- Supplier ownership structure documentation
- Product origin certificates

### 5.3 CAPITAL EXPENDITURE (Asset Depreciation)

#### Inclusion Criteria
1. **Assets manufactured in KSA** → 100% of depreciation counted as LC
2. **Assets imported from outside KSA** → 20% of depreciation counted as LC
3. **Assets located in KSA** → eligible for consideration
4. **Assets used in business operations** → eligible

#### Exclusion Criteria
1. Assets located outside KSA
2. Leased assets (treat lease payments as services)
3. Intangible assets (software licenses, patents, goodwill)
4. Assets not yet put into use
5. Low-value assets (below capitalization threshold)
6. Financial assets (investments, securities)
7. Inventory/inventory-like assets

#### Asset Classifications
| Asset Type | KSA-Made Factor | Imported Factor |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Buildings & Structures | 100% | 20% |
| Machinery & Equipment | 100% | 20% |
| Vehicles & Transportation | 100% | 20% |
| IT Equipment & Hardware | 100% | 20% |
| Furniture & Fixtures | 100% | 20% |
| Tools & Instruments | 100% | 20% |
| Leasehold Improvements | 100% | N/A (local construction) |

#### Required Evidence
- Fixed asset register (detailed)
- Asset purchase invoices
- Origin certificates/manufacturer statements
- Depreciation schedules
- Asset location documentation
- Proof of asset use in KSA operations
- For local assets: manufacturer declaration and proof of KSA manufacture

### 5.4 CAPACITY BUILDING

#### Eligible Categories

| Category | Description | Documentation |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Employee Training | Training programs for Saudi employees | Training records, certificates, invoices |
| Supplier Development | Programs to develop local suppliers | Supplier contracts, development plans |
| Research & Development | R&D activities conducted in KSA | R&D project records, lab expenses |
| Technology Transfer | Knowledge transfer programs | MOUs, license agreements |
| University Collaboration | Partnerships with Saudi universities | Agreements, research output |
| Scholarship Programs | Scholarships for Saudi students | Scholarship contracts, enrollment proof |
| Internship Programs | Saudi intern/co-op programs | Intern contracts, university agreements |
| Quality Certification | SASO/ISO certification costs | Certificates, audit reports |

#### Eligibility Rules
1. Must be conducted within KSA (or directly benefit Saudi entities)
2. Must be incremental to normal business operations
3. Must have verifiable outputs/outcomes
4. Training must be for Saudi nationals (or mixed groups pro-rated)
5. R&D must have defined objectives and results

#### Ineligible Expenses
- General administrative overhead
- Marketing and sales expenses
- Legal and professional fees (unless specified)
- Entertainment and hospitality
- Fines and penalties
- Political contributions

### 5.5 DEPRECIATION & AMORTIZATION — Detailed Rules

#### Local Asset Requirements
1. Asset must be manufactured in KSA
2. Manufacturing must involve substantial transformation
3. Manufacturer must have valid industrial license
4. Asset must be owned (not leased)
5. Asset must be physically located in KSA
6. Depreciation method must follow GAAP/SOCPA
7. Asset life must be reasonable and documented

#### Imported Asset Treatment
1. Only 20% of depreciation/amortization is recognized as LC
2. Import customs documentation required
3. Asset must be registered in KSA
4. Must be used in KSA operations
5. Import duties and shipping costs may be treated separately

## 6. PHASE 5: AUDIT REQUIREMENTS

### 6.1 Agreed Upon Procedures (AUP) Framework

The AUP is a licensed auditor's engagement to perform specific procedures agreed between LCGPA and the entity, without expressing an audit opinion.

**Objective:** Verify the accuracy and completeness of the local content data submitted

**Standard:** International Standard on Related Services (ISRS) 4400 / SOCPA equivalent

### 6.2 AUP Scope

The AUP engagement covers verification of:

1. **Entity Information** — Legal name, CR number, ownership structure
2. **Financial Statements** — Accuracy and completeness
3. **Workforce Data** — Employee count, nationality, salaries, GOSI
4. **Supplier Spend** — Local vs imported procurement classification
5. **Asset Register** — Asset origin and depreciation calculations
6. **Capacity Building** — Eligibility and verification of CB expenses
7. **Local Content Calculation** — Accuracy of formula application
8. **Supporting Documentation** — Completeness and authenticity

### 6.3 Audit Methodology

1. **Planning Phase**
   - Understand entity operations and data systems
   - Assess data completeness and reliability
   - Determine materiality thresholds
   - Design specific procedures

2. **Execution Phase**
   - Test financial data against source documents
   - Verify employee data against GOSI and Iqama records
   - Test supplier classification against ownership docs
   - Verify asset origin through manufacturer declarations
   - Sample transactions for detailed testing

3. **Reporting Phase**
   - Prepare AUP report with findings
   - Report any exceptions or limitations
   - Submit to LCGPA via portal
   - Maintain working papers for 7+ years

### 6.4 Sampling Methodology

| Category | Sampling Approach | Coverage |
|----------|------------------|----------|
| Payroll | Statistical or judgmental | High-value employees + random sample |
| Procurement | Stratified by value | All transactions above threshold + sample of low-value |
| Assets | 100% for high-value | All assets above materiality threshold |
| Capacity Building | 100% review | All CB expenditures |
| Supplier Declarations | Representative sample | Based on supplier categories |

### 6.5 Materiality Thresholds

While LCGPA does not publish explicit materiality thresholds, industry practice (from Grant Thornton, BMS, and other auditors) suggests:

- **High risk:** Single transaction >5% of total LC score
- **Medium risk:** Transaction between 1-5% of total LC score
- **Low risk:** Transaction <1% of total LC score

### 6.6 Required Audit Evidence

| Evidence Type | Purpose | Format |
|--------------|---------|--------|
| Audited FS | Financial data verification | PDF/Excel |
| GOSI Certificates | Employee Saudi status | Official printout |
| Iqama Copies | Non-Saudi verification | Copies/PDF |
| Commercial Registration | Entity legal status | CR copy |
| Industrial License | Manufacturer status | License copy |
| Supplier Declarations | Supplier classification | Signed form |
| Invoices | Spend verification | PDF/paper |
| Customs Documents | Import verification | Customs declarations |
| Asset Register | Asset listing | Excel/ERP export |
| Depreciation Schedule | Asset calculation | Excel/accounting |
| Training Records | CB verification | Certificates, attendance |
| R&D Reports | CB verification | Project documentation |
| Bank Statements | Payment verification | Bank PDF |
| Contracts | Commercial agreements | Signed contracts |

### 6.7 Audit Reporting Requirements

The AUP report must include:

1. **Entity Information** — Name, CR number, fiscal year
2. **Summary of Procedures Performed** — What was tested
3. **Findings** — Detailed results of each procedure
4. **Exceptions** — Any deviations or missing documentation
5. **Calculated LC Score** — Final percentage
6. **Component Breakdown** — Each component's contribution
7. **Auditor Declaration** — Independence and competence
8. **Date and Signature** — Licensed auditor's signature

### 10.3 Workflows

#### Workflow 1: LC Certificate Application
```
1. Entity registers in LocalContentOS
2. Entity uploads company documents
3. Entity imports HR data (manual/API)
4. Entity imports procurement data (manual/API)
5. Entity imports asset data (manual/API)
6. Entity records CB activities
7. System calculates provisional LC score
8. Internal review and validation
9. External auditor assigned
10. Auditor performs AUP procedures
11. AUP report generated in system
12. Report submitted to LCGPA portal
13. LCGPA reviews and approves
14. Certificate issued (valid 19 months)
15. Certificate stored and tracked in system
```

#### Workflow 2: Mandatory List Compliance
```
1. System ingests mandatory list updates
2. Entity procurement data checked against list
3. Flag if procuring listed products from non-local sources
4. Exception workflow initiated if needed
5. Compliance report generated
6. Management review and action
```

#### Workflow 3: AUP Audit
```
1. Audit engagement created in system
2. Procedures defined based on LCGPA requirements
3. Sampling methodology selected
4. Evidence requests sent to entity
5. Entity uploads evidence via portal
6. Auditor reviews and tests evidence
7. Exceptions documented
8. Audit working papers generated
9. AUP report drafted and reviewed
10. Report signed and submitted
```

#### Workflow 4: Continuous Monitoring
```
1. Monthly data import from entity ERP
2. Automated LC score calculation
3. Variance analysis (month-over-month)
4. Alert if score drops below target
5. Trend analysis and projection
6. Management dashboard updated
7. Quarterly compliance report
```

## 13. PHASE 12: EXECUTIVE ASSESSMENT

### 13.1 Key Insights

1. **LCGPA is rapidly expanding its regulatory scope** — From government procurement to SOEs (Resolution 658), and now introducing minimum LC thresholds on mandatory list products (Feb 2026). This is a clear signal of strengthening enforcement.

2. **The market is under-digitized** — Most companies still use Excel templates and manual processes for LC calculations. This creates a massive product opportunity for LocalContentOS.

3. **Separation of LCGPA and IKTVA creates dual compliance burden** — Companies supplying both government and Aramco need separate systems, separate audits, separate reports. This is a key market position for LocalContentOS (unified platform for both frameworks).

4. **Certificate validity (19 months) creates recurring revenue model** — Companies must recertify every 19 months, creating predictable recurring revenue for compliance software.

5. **Mandatory list expansion (400+ products and growing) creates continuous compliance need** — As more products are added, companies need ongoing monitoring and compliance tools.

6. **AUP audit bottleneck** — Limited number of licensed auditors creates a bottleneck. AI-assisted audit tools can significantly accelerate the process.

7. **Data integration is the critical success factor** — Companies have data in multiple ERP systems. The ability to integrate seamlessly will determine adoption.

### 13.2 Hidden Requirements

1. **GOSI real-time verification** — Not explicitly required in documents but implied by data validation needs
2. **ZATCA customs data integration** — For verifying imported goods declarations
3. **SASO certification cross-reference** — For mandatory list product compliance
4. **Ministry of Industry license verification** — For manufacturer status verification
5. **Geographic asset tracking** — Assets must be in KSA; location verification needed
6. **Beneficial ownership verification** — For determining Saudi ownership percentage
7. **Subcontractor/supplier chain tracking** — Not currently required but emerging
8. **Environmental/Sustainability linkage** — Expected as LC framework matures

### 13.3 Common Compliance Failures (Based on Auditor Reports)

1. **Incorrect supplier classification** — Labeling distributors as manufacturers
2. **Expatriate salary miscounting** — Treating 100% instead of 37% for non-Saudis
3. **Imported asset misclassification** — Claiming 100% LC for imported assets
4. **Missing GOSI for Saudi employees** — Required for 100% LC claim
5. **Manufacturer declaration not obtained** — Required for local product classification
6. **Inconsistent data across systems** — Payroll vs GOSI, Procurement vs AP
7. **Capacity building ineligible expenses** — Including non-qualifying activities
8. **Outdated CR/Industrial licenses** — Changes in ownership not updated
9. **Expired certificates not renewed** — Certificate validity lapse
10. **Incomplete audit evidence** — Missing invoices, contracts, declarations
