# Reviewer Trust Assessment

## Does governance increase reviewer confidence?

Yes. The presence of governance signals — particularly the DraftOnlyBanner and the Governance Context Panel — provided the reviewer with a clear framework for evaluating the AI-generated content. Knowing which doctrine references and governance rules applied to each statement reduced ambiguity and allowed the reviewer to focus on substantive evaluation rather than wondering about provenance or applicable standards.

## Does reviewer understand AI limitations?

Yes. The "human review required" language across multiple signals explicitly communicates that the AI output is not final and may contain errors. The reviewer did not treat AI-generated findings as authoritative. The reviewer cross-referenced AI assertions against evidence rather than accepting them at face value.

## Does reviewer understand human accountability remains theirs?

Yes. Every governance signal reinforces that final approval rests with the human reviewer. The DraftOnlyBanner states "Human review required," the Governance Context Panel repeats this, and the inline text near finalization actions restates it. The reviewer's behavior indicated understanding — they read each finding critically and did not delegate judgment to the system.

## Is there risk of over-trust?

No. The current governance layer is designed to prevent over-trust through:
- Prominent draft-state indicators
- Explicit human-review requirements
- Verifiable provenance citations
- No automated approval paths

The reviewer did not exhibit any behavior suggesting over-reliance on AI output.

## Is there risk of under-trust?

Low. The governance signals provide enough transparency that the reviewer can verify the AI's work. If governance signals were absent or sparse, there would be a risk of under-trust (reviewer distrusting the entire system). Current signal density is sufficient for confidence without being overwhelming.

## Conclusion

The governance layer creates healthy trust calibration. The reviewer trusts the governance signals themselves (they are consistent, verifiable, and transparent) while maintaining appropriate skepticism toward the AI-generated content. Human accountability is clearly communicated and was observed in practice.
