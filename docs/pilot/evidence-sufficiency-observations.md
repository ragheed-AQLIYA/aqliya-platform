# Evidence Sufficiency Observations — Real TB Intake

## Evidence Types Encountered
| Type | Frequency | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Bank statements | High | Primary source for cash, most reliable |
| Invoices | High | Often mismatched with revenue entries |
| Contracts | Medium | Used for revenue recognition, loan terms |
| Confirmations | Low | Third-party confirmations rare |
| Internal ledgers | Medium | Used as fallback when source docs missing |
| Screenshots | Low | Ad-hoc evidence, questionable reliability |

## Sufficiency by Account Area
| Account Area | Sufficiency Rating | Reasoning |
|-------------|-------------------|-----------|
| Cash | High | Bank statements readily available, direct matching |
| Receivables | Medium | Invoices available but aging schedules often missing |
| Inventory | Low | Physical counts absent, valuation docs incomplete |
| PPE | Medium | Purchase invoices exist, depreciation schedules inconsistent |
| Loans | High | Contracts present, repayment schedules traceable |
| Revenue | Low | Recognition criteria documentation sparse, cut-off issues |

## Missing Evidence Patterns
- Inventory count sheets absent in 3/5 engagements
- Revenue cut-off support missing at period boundaries
- Related-party transaction documentation absent
- Subsequent receipts not gathered for receivables
- Bank confirmations omitted entirely (cost/time reasons)

## Weak Evidence Patterns
- Internal reconciliations used as audit evidence without external corroboration
- Management representations as sole evidence for estimates
- Screenshots of system data without traceability to source
- Signed contracts missing key terms (interest rates, payment schedules)
- Handwritten amendments on invoices without countersignature

## Conflicting Evidence Patterns
- Bank statement balance vs. GL balance (timing differences unresolved)
- Invoice amounts vs. contract amounts (change orders not documented)
- Revenue per sales report vs. revenue per bank deposit
- Loan balance per contract vs. per repayment schedule (fee capitalisation)

## Reviewer Judgment Dependency
- Inventory valuation accepted without physical verification — judgment call
- Revenue cut-off accepted based on management rep alone — risk accepted
- PPE useful life assumptions accepted without industry benchmarking
- Provision adequacy accepted without third-party legal confirmation
- **Pattern**: Judgment dependencies cluster around areas where evidence cost exceeds perceived risk

## Governance/Provenance Usefulness During Evidence Assessment
- Provenance tags helped identify which evidence was client-supplied vs. system-generated
- Source attribution reduced re-verification time for high-sufficiency areas (cash, loans)
- Low usefulness for inventory and revenue — provenance recorded but evidence still insufficient
- Governance panel gave quick visibility into missing docs but did not solve the sufficiency gap
- **Net assessment**: Provenance useful for triage (knowing where to look), not for resolution (actual evidence still needed)
