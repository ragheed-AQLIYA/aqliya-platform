# Founder Principles Index

## Purpose
Index the founding principles of AQLIYA as established in Part 01 — Foundational Doctrine. Each entry traces a principle to its source document, defines its meaning, and notes how it evolved.

---

### 1. Decisions Are Infrastructure

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principle** | Decisions — their evidence, reasoning, approval, and outcome — are first-class enterprise assets, not ephemeral events. They must be stored, traced, and learnable. |
| **Source Document** | 01.01 AQLIYA Foundational Thesis, §6 Core Principles (1) |
| **Meaning** | Decisions are structured objects with identity, schema, lifecycle, storage, access control, and versioning — the same structural properties as financial transactions. The decision object is the architectural center of the platform. |
| **Evolution** | Originated as a core principle in 01.01. Expanded into Decision Infrastructure Theory (02.02) where decisions are formalized as objects with context, evidence, options, recommendation, approval, action, outcome, and learning. Became the architectural thesis for the entire platform. |

### 2. Intelligence Is Earned, Not Assumed

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principle** | Trust in AI outputs must be built through evidence, explainability, and demonstrated reliability over time. No output is trusted by default. |
| **Source Document** | 01.01 AQLIYA Foundational Thesis, §6 Core Principles (2) |
| **Meaning** | Trust is not granted by marketing claims or model benchmarks. It is accumulated through repeated positive outcomes — one evidence trace, one accurate anomaly, one helpful recommendation at a time. |
| **Evolution** | Foundation principle in 01.01. Formalized into the Progressive Intelligence Model in 13.05 (Intelligence Before Automation Thesis) and the Explainability Before Autonomy Thesis (13.06). Now governs the staged deployment of all AI capabilities. |

### 3. Workflows Are the Substrate

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principle** | Intelligence, evidence, governance, and decisions live within workflows. Workflows are not overhead — they are the structure that makes intelligence useful and accountable. |
| **Source Document** | 01.01 AQLIYA Foundational Thesis, §6 Core Principles (3) |
| **Meaning** | The workflow is the primary user interface and the architectural substrate. Dashboards are secondary views derived from workflow state. The entire platform is a workflow execution environment. |
| **Evolution** | Originated in 01.01 §6 and §5 (Workflow-first philosophy). Expanded into dedicated philosophy document 01.08 (Workflow-First Company Philosophy) and product thesis 13.04 (Workflow Before Dashboard Thesis). Became a category-defining architectural position distinguishing AQLIYA from dashboard and chatbot competitors. |

### 4. Governance Is Structural, Not Procedural

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principle** | Governance is built into the system, not applied as a layer on top. If governance can be bypassed, it will be. |
| **Source Document** | 01.01 AQLIYA Foundational Thesis, §6 Core Principles (4) |
| **Meaning** | Governance is enforced by system architecture — the workflow engine, data model, and intelligence layer — not by policy documents, user training, or compliance checklists. Governance rules execute as synchronous guards at workflow transitions. |
| **Evolution** | Core principle in 01.01. Developed into full philosophy document 01.07 (Governance-First Company Philosophy) which establishes governance as "the operating system of the platform, not a feature module." Further refined in 08.01 (Governance & Trust Thesis) and 18.04 (Governance-Less AI Anti-Pattern) which makes the structural governance position absolute. |

### 5. The Human Is Accountable

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principle** | AI can recommend, assist, alert, and explain. But the professional human reviewer remains accountable for every decision. The system serves the reviewer, not the reverse. |
| **Source Document** | 01.01 AQLIYA Foundational Thesis, §6 Core Principles (5) |
| **Meaning** | In regulated, liability-bearing domains, human accountability is non-negotiable. No system output may be treated as a final decision without attributable human authority. AI operates in an assistive role only. |
| **Evolution** | Core principle in 01.01 (also stated as "Human-in-the-loop, always" in §5). Formalized in 10.01 (Human + AI Thesis), 15.01 (Responsible Intelligence Doctrine), and 15.04 (No-Autonomous-Audit Decision Rule). Now enforced as an architectural constraint — human decision joints cannot be bypassed by automation. |

### 6. Evidence Is the Unit of Trust

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principle** | Every intelligence output must be reducible to the evidence that supports it. Evidence is not optional metadata — it is the core data type. Data becomes evidence only when it has context, provenance, and relevance. |
| **Source Document** | 01.01 AQLIYA Foundational Thesis, §6 Core Principles (6) |
| **Meaning** | Raw data is not evidence. Evidence is data that has been validated, attributed, and positioned within a decision context. The distinction between data and evidence is the foundation of trust in the system. |
| **Evolution** | Core principle in 01.01 (also stated in §5 as "Evidence-centric"). Developed into full philosophy document 01.09 (Evidence-Centric Company Philosophy) which establishes evidence as a first-class data type with its own schema, lifecycle, storage, and governance. |

### 7. Auditability Is Non-Negotiable

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principle** | Every action, recommendation, and decision must be auditable after the fact. If it cannot be audited, it should not have been done. |
| **Source Document** | 01.01 AQLIYA Foundational Thesis, §6 Core Principles (7) |
| **Meaning** | Audit trails are not hidden in logs — they are visible, navigable, and comprehensible to authorized users. Auditability is a structural property of the workflow engine (event sourcing, immutable state transitions). |
| **Evolution** | Core principle in 01.01. Expanded in 02.05 (Decision Traceability Theory), 08.03 (Auditability Doctrine), and 08.05 (Traceability Doctrine). Now embedded as an architectural guarantee through event sourcing patterns. |

### 8. Simplicity Through Structure

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principle** | Complex domains require structured systems, not complex interfaces. The system should be simple because the structure is right, not because complexity is hidden. |
| **Source Document** | 01.01 AQLIYA Foundational Thesis, §6 Core Principles (8) |
| **Meaning** | Structure enables simplicity. The product is opinionated — it guides reviewers through structured processes rather than presenting an empty canvas. Simplicity is the result of disciplined structure. |
| **Evolution** | Core principle in 01.01. Became a product design principle in 13.01 (Product Philosophy Thesis) §6(2): "Structure enables simplicity." Further developed in 13.02 (Product Simplicity Philosophy). |

### 9. Domain Depth Over Horizontal Breadth

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principle** | AQLIYA wins by going deep into specific decision-intensive domains — audit first, then adjacent financial and governance domains — not by being a generic platform. |
| **Source Document** | 01.01 AQLIYA Foundational Thesis, §6 Core Principles (9) |
| **Meaning** | The first defensible moat is Financial Intelligence. Category breadth without domain depth is marketing, not strategy. Platform companies die when they expand before they dominate their initial wedge. |
| **Evolution** | Core principle in 01.01. Formalized as an anti-pattern in 18.06 (Premature Platform Expansion Anti-Pattern). Refined in 01.03 (What AQLIYA Is / Is Not) where AuditOS is defined as a product line and wedge, not the company identity. |

### 10. Long-Term Category Value Over Short-Term Revenue

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principle** | Decisions that weaken the category thesis — like becoming a generic AI tool or a low-margin SaaS feature — are rejected even if they generate short-term revenue. |
| **Source Document** | 01.01 AQLIYA Foundational Thesis, §6 Core Principles (10) |
| **Meaning** | A deal won through mispositioning is a category loss even if it is a revenue win. The revenue is temporary; the positioning damage is permanent. |
| **Evolution** | Core principle in 01.01. Expanded into the seven-question Positioning Test in 01.03 §6. Reinforced in 13.01 (Product Philosophy Thesis) anti-patterns and 13.12 (Product Focus Doctrine). Becomes the binding rule for all product and commercial decisions. |

### 11. What AQLIYA Is / Is Not (Identity Boundaries)

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principle** | Identity is strategy. What AQLIYA refuses to be is as important as what it builds. Every "no" protects the category thesis. |
| **Source Document** | 01.03 What AQLIYA Is / Is Not, §6 Core Principles |
| **Meaning** | AQLIYA is: AI operating systems for governed enterprise work, an evidence-centric decision system, a human-in-the-loop infrastructure, a category creator. AQLIYA is not: a chatbot, a generic AI copilot, a dashboard or BI tool, an ERP, a CRM, an audit checklist tool, an automation agency, a prompt layer, a generic BPM tool, a feature factory. |
| **Evolution** | Derived from 01.01 and expanded into a standalone identity definition document. The Is/Is Not Matrix and seven-question Positioning Test provide concrete filters for every product, sales, and partnership decision. This is the binding guard against identity drift. |

### 12. Governance-First Company Philosophy

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principle** | Governance is not a feature module. Governance is the substrate on which all other capabilities operate. |
| **Source Document** | 01.07 Governance-First Company Philosophy, §2 Thesis |
| **Meaning** | Every workflow executes within governance boundaries enforced by the system, not by user compliance. Governance rules cannot be bypassed by any user role. "No anonymous action" — every action attributed to an authenticated actor. |
| **Evolution** | Started as a philosophical commitment in 01.01 §5. Dedicated philosophy document 01.07 formalized it as a design constraint. Governance became "the operating system of the platform" per 01.07 §5, further embedded in 08.01 (Governance & Trust Thesis). The anti-pattern 18.04 (Governance-Less AI) codifies the prohibition on shipping AI without governance. |

### 13. Workflow-First Company Philosophy

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principle** | The workflow is the interface. The workflow is the system. Intelligence, evidence, governance, and decisions all live within workflows. |
| **Source Document** | 01.08 Workflow-First Company Philosophy, §2 Thesis |
| **Meaning** | AQLIYA does not have workflows. AQLIYA is workflows. The primary user experience is the structured progression of evidence, review, recommendation, approval, and action. Dashboards are secondary views computed from workflow state. |
| **Evolution** | From philosophical commitment in 01.01 §5. Dedicated philosophy document 01.08 formalizes workflow as the primary organizational and product paradigm. Product thesis 13.04 (Workflow Before Dashboard) makes this a category-defining architectural decision. |

### 14. Evidence-Centric Company Philosophy

| Field | Content |
|---|---|
| **Principle** | Evidence is the central data type and organizing principle of the platform. Data becomes evidence only when it has context, provenance, and relevance to a specific decision or finding. |
| **Source Document** | 01.09 Evidence-Centric Company Philosophy, §2 Thesis |
| **Meaning** | Evidence has a defined schema, lifecycle, storage, access controls, and governance rules. It is not metadata attached to another object. The quality of decisions is bounded by the quality of evidence. |
| **Evolution** | From philosophical commitment in 01.01 §5 and §6(6). Dedicated philosophy document 01.09 establishes evidence as a first-class data type. Developed into the Evidence Store architectural component in 02.02 (Decision Infrastructure Theory) and the Evidence Lifecycle Framework in 07.06. |
