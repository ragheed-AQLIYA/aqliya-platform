# Financial Intelligence — Operating Model

Maps Financial Intelligence capabilities from Part 04 (Financial Intelligence) and Part 09 (Data Trust) into a competitive operating moat.

## Capability–Doctrine–Advantage–Product Map

| Capability | Doctrine Source | Competitive Advantage | Product Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Revenue Attribution | Part 04 §4.2 — Revenue as Evidence | Every dollar of revenue is traceable to a specific decision outcome and evidence chain — audit-proof billing | Product shows revenue-per-decision; clients see ROI at the individual decision level |
| Cost of Evidence | Part 04 §4.4 — Evidence Costing | Full cost allocation per evidence acquisition, processing, and retention — enables transparent pricing | Cost-by-evidence-class dashboard; clients can optimise evidence spend |
| Data Trust Valuation | Part 09 §9.3 — Trust as Asset | Data Trust agreements are capitalised as intangible assets; trustworthiness directly impacts valuation | Data Trust Score visible to clients; higher score = lower risk premium |
| Financial Audit Trail | Part 04 §4.6 — Financial Traceability | All financial flows (revenue, cost, royalty) are on-chain or cryptographically attested | CFO dashboard with real-time financial audit trail; no reconciliation needed |
| Royalty & Revenue Share | Part 09 §9.5 — Value Distribution | Automated royalty distribution per Data Trust terms; auditable and non-repudiable | Smart contract triggers payment on evidence usage; partners see real-time share |
| Pricing Model Integrity | Part 04 §4.8 — Pricing Doctrine | Pricing is tied to doctrine-defined value metrics — cannot be arbitrarily discounted without governance approval | Pricing Engine enforces minimums; any override logged for board review |
| Financial Risk Scoring | Part 09 §9.7 — Trust Risk | Client financial health and data trust compliance produce a composite risk score | Risk score gates credit terms, pilot conversion, and contract renewal |
| Capital Efficiency Ratio | Part 04 §4.3 — Efficiency Doctrine | Ratio of evidence value to cost of evidence is the primary efficiency metric | Product reports efficiency ratio per client, per system, per decision class |

```
    │

    ┌─────────────────────────────────────┐

```

## How the Moat Defends

| Competitive Threat | Moat Response | Doctrine Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Price undercutting | Evidence-cost-based pricing is the floor; doctrine prohibits below-cost | Part 04 §4.8 |
| Audit disputes | Every revenue line has a cryptographic evidence chain | Part 04 §4.2, §4.6 |
| Partner churn | Automated royalty distribution builds trust and reduces friction | Part 09 §9.5 |
| Client credit risk | Risk score gates exposure before losses materialise | Part 09 §9.7 |
| Regulatory challenge | Full financial audit trail satisfies regulatory demands out of the box | Part 04 §4.6 |
| Competitor copying | Doctrine-integrated pricing and trust model is structural, not feature-based | Part 04, Part 09 foundations |

## Governance Implications

- Financial Intelligence is the only system that spans both evidence (AuditOS) and trust (Data Trust)
- CFO must certify financial audit trail integrity quarterly
- Pricing overrides require both Commercial Officer and Doctrine Steader approval
- Data Trust valuation is reviewed annually against doctrine updates
