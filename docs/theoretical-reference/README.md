# AQLIYA Theoretical Reference System

## What This Is

This is the structured intellectual foundation of AQLIYA. It is not generic documentation — it is a long-term reference system that defines the company's theory, philosophy, and principles across 21 parts.

## Doctrine Modernization Note

The theoretical reference system was originally authored when Enterprise Decision Intelligence language served as AQLIYA's top-level category framing.

Under the official company architecture, AQLIYA is a company/platform with multiple product lines. Within this corpus, phrases such as `Enterprise Decision Intelligence`, `decision infrastructure`, and similar doctrine language should be interpreted as one strategic doctrine and product-domain thesis inside AQLIYA, not as the full company identity.

Likewise, references to AuditOS as the "first wedge" should now be read as historical doctrine language for AQLIYA's first commercial focus and current primary product line.

Every document here exists to anchor product decisions, architecture choices, market positioning, governance rules, and strategic narratives in a coherent, evidence-driven intellectual framework.

## Why It Exists

AQLIYA is not building a feature. AQLIYA is building a company platform of AI operating systems, with Enterprise Decision Intelligence remaining one important strategic doctrine inside that broader architecture.

Without a structured theoretical foundation, the company risks:
- Drifting into random feature-building
- Losing coherence across product, engineering, and GTM
- Being unable to defend its positioning with enterprise buyers
- Becoming an "AI wrapper" instead of an intelligence platform

This reference system exists to prevent that drift.

## How to Use This System

1. **Start with Part 01 (Foundational Doctrine)** — understand why AQLIYA exists
2. **Follow the writing agenda** — documents are ordered by dependency
3. **Every document uses the standard template** — consistency is mandatory
4. **New documents must have an ID and belong to a part** — no random docs
5. **All major updates must be logged** — version history is required

## Rules for Adding New Documents

- Every document must have a unique ID (e.g., 01.01)
- Every document must belong to one of the 21 parts
- No document outside the master index
- New terms must be added to Part 17 (Terminology & Definitions)
- Every theory must connect to product, architecture, governance, or market narrative
- Every major update must be logged in version history
- Every document must have a status (Not Started, Draft, Reviewed, Approved, Active Reference, Needs Revision, Deprecated)
- Delete or defer documents that do not affect product, architecture, governance, AI, UX, or commercialization
