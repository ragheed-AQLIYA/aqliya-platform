# Evidence-to-Principle Query Map

## Core Rule

> **Evidence supports reasoning. Evidence does not remove human responsibility.**

Every output produced by any system in this ecosystem — whether assistive, recommendatory, or autonomous — derives its authority from human professional judgement. Evidence strengthens that judgement; it never replaces it. The human professional remains accountable for all conclusions, regardless of the volume or quality of evidence assembled.

---

## 1. Evidence Concept Definition

**Evidence** is any information — qualitative or quantitative, documentary or observational — that supports or contradicts a proposition, assertion, finding, or conclusion. In the professional services context, evidence serves as the factual substrate upon which reasoning operates.

| Attribute | Definition |
|-----------|------------|
| **Sufficiency** | The quantity of evidence needed to support a conclusion at a given level of assurance. Sufficiency is a matter of professional judgement. |
| **Appropriateness** | The quality of evidence — its relevance to the proposition and its reliability as a representation of fact. |
| **Provenance** | The origin and chain of custody of the evidence item, establishing its authenticity and integrity. |
| **Persistence** | Evidence must be retained in a tamper-evident form for the duration of the required retention period. |

Evidence that is insufficient or inappropriate cannot support a conclusion, regardless of the reasoning applied to it.

---

## 2. Evidence Types

| Type | Description | Reliability | Examples |
|------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| **Direct Documentary** | Source documents generated by the entity or third parties that directly support a fact. | High (third-party) / Medium (entity-generated) | Invoices, bank statements, contracts, confirmations |
| **Computational** | Evidence generated through calculation, reconciliation, or model output. | Medium–High (depends on model validation) | Reperformance calculations, model projections, ratio analysis |
| **Observational** | Evidence obtained through direct observation of processes, controls, or physical assets. | High (at time of observation) | Inventory counts, control walkthroughs, system demonstrations |
| **Inquiry** | Representations made by management, staff, or third parties in response to questions. | Low–Medium (requires corroboration) | Management representations, expert interviews, client questionnaires |
| **Analytical** | Evidence derived from analysis of relationships among data. | Medium (depends on data quality and analytical method) | Trend analysis, ratio analysis, predictive modelling |
| **External Confirmatory** | Evidence obtained directly from independent third parties. | High | Bank confirmations, legal letters, customer confirmations |
| **System-Generated** | Evidence produced automatically by information systems. | Medium–High (depends on system controls) | Audit logs, access records, automated reconciliation outputs |

---

## 3. Evidence-to-Principle Mapping

Each category of evidence maps to the governing doctrinal principles that determine its acceptable use:

| Evidence Type | Governing Principle(s) | Principle Reference | Constraint |
|---------------|----------------------|---------------------|------------|
| Direct Documentary | Verifiability, Objectivity | DP-01, DP-02 | Must be cross-referenced to source. Photocopies and scans require authenticity verification. |
| Computational | Accuracy, Reproducibility | DP-03, DP-07 | Models must be validated. Assumptions must be disclosed. Reperformance must yield identical results. |
| Observational | Timeliness, Independence | DP-04, DP-08 | Observations are point-in-time. Changes after observation are not captured unless re-observed. |
| Inquiry | Corroboration, Professional Skepticism | DP-02, DP-05 | Inquiry alone is never sufficient. All inquiry evidence must be corroborated by at least one other evidence type. |
| Analytical | Relevance, Materiality | DP-06, DP-09 | Analytical procedures must target identified risks. Deviations from expectation require explanation. |
| External Confirmatory | Independence, Directness | DP-08, DP-10 | The confirming party must be truly independent. Responses must be received directly, not routed through the client. |
| System-Generated | Integrity, Continuity | DP-11, DP-12 | System controls must be tested before relying on system-generated evidence. Log integrity must be verifiable. |

## 4. Evidence-to-Governance Mapping

Evidence items must comply with the governance framework applicable to the engagement type:

| Governance Domain | Evidence Requirement | Policy Reference | Non-Compliance Consequence |
|-------------------|---------------------|------------------|---------------------------|
| **Audit Governance** | Evidence must meet ISA 500 (Audit Evidence) standards of sufficiency and appropriateness. | G-AUD-01 | Draft findings cannot advance to final. |
| **Accounting Governance** | Evidence must support the recognition and measurement criteria of the applicable financial reporting framework. | G-ACC-01 | Journal entries cannot be posted. |
| **AI Governance** | Evidence of model validation, bias testing, and human review must be retained for every AI-assisted output. | G-AI-04 | AI outputs remain draft and cannot be released. |
| **Product Governance** | Evidence of design decisions, capability baselines, and test coverage must exist for every commercial claim. | G-PRD-02 | Claims are unregistered and cannot be made. |
| **Risk Governance** | Evidence of risk assessment, mitigation design, and residual risk acceptance must be documented. | G-RSK-01 | Risk acceptance is void; risk is treated as unmitigated. |

## 5. Evidence-to-Workflow Mapping

Evidence attaches to specific workflow stages. A workflow stage cannot complete until its evidence requirements are satisfied:

| Workflow Stage | Required Evidence | Blocking Condition | Release Gate |
|----------------|-------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Scoping** | Engagement letter, risk profile, materiality determination | Missing scope document | Scoping Approval |
| **Planning** | Risk assessment, materiality, sampling methodology | Unapproved methodology | Planning Approval |
| **Execution** | Procedure outputs, work papers, exception logs | Incomplete procedures | Execution Review |
| **Review** | Reviewer notes, cleared review points, sign-offs | Outstanding review points | Review Sign-off |
| **Reporting** | Final output, evidence index, approval record | Missing evidence chain | Final Approval |
| **Archival** | Complete evidence register, chain of custody, retention confirmation | Incomplete register | Archival Confirmation |

## 6. AuditOS Examples

### Example 1: Substantive Testing — Accounts Receivable Confirmation

```
Query: "Confirm the accounts receivable balance for Customer C-42 as at 31 December."
Evidence Assembled: Bank confirmation E-01, Customer response E-02, Invoice register E-03
Principle Applied: DP-08 (Independence: external confirmation required)
Governance: G-AUD-01 (ISA 505 External Confirmations)
Workflow Stage: Execution
AuditOS Output: "Balance of $142,000 confirmed via external confirmation E-02. Evidence chain complete. Draft finding: AR balance for C-42 is fairly stated."
Boundary: Draft only. Final opinion requires Reviewer sign-off.
```

### Example 2: Control Testing — Segregation of Duties

```
Query: "Test whether segregation of duties exists between purchase order creation and approval."
Evidence Assembled: System access logs E-04, Control matrix E-05, Inquiry response E-06
Principle Applied: DP-02 (Objectivity: inquiry must be corroborated)
Governance: G-AUD-01 (ISA 315 Control Testing)
Workflow Stage: Execution
AuditOS Output: "Inquiry E-06 states segregation is maintained. System logs E-04 confirm distinct user roles for creation (U-101) and approval (U-202). Control is operating effectively for the period tested. Inquiry corroborated by system-generated evidence."
Boundary: Draft finding. Requires control testing reviewer sign-off.
```

### Example 3: AI-Assisted Risk Assessment

```
Query: "Generate risk assessment for engagement E-55 based on trial balance data."
Evidence Assembled: Trial balance E-07, Prior year work papers E-08, Industry benchmarks E-09, Model validation record E-10
Principle Applied: DP-06 (Relevance: analytical procedures must target identified risks)
Governance: G-AI-04 (AI output is recommendatory only; human review required)
Workflow Stage: Planning
AuditOS Output: "Based on analytical procedures, accounts with variance > 15% from expectation are flagged for further audit attention: Revenue (22% above expectation), Cost of Sales (18% above expectation). Model validation record E-10 confirms model operated within approved tolerances. This is a recommendatory output."
Boundary: Recommendatory draft. Engagement Lead must review and approve the risk assessment before it becomes the basis for the audit plan.
```

## 7. Missing Evidence Handling

When required evidence is unavailable, incomplete, or of insufficient quality, the following protocol applies:

| Scenario | Response | Escalation | Output Boundary |
|----------|----------|------------|-----------------|
| **Evidence cannot be obtained** | Document the limitation. Assess whether alternative procedures can provide sufficient appropriate evidence. | Escalate to Engagement Lead if the limitation is material. | The affected finding must include a scope limitation note. The output cannot advance beyond draft without the Engagement Lead's written acceptance of the limitation. |
| **Evidence is contradictory** | Perform additional procedures to resolve the contradiction. Document the resolution. | Escalate if contradiction cannot be resolved and is material. | The finding must disclose the contradiction and the resolution steps. If unresolved, the output is locked as draft. |
| **Evidence quality is insufficient** | Assess whether supplementary evidence can raise quality to the required threshold. | Escalate to Technical Review if quality cannot be remediated. | The finding must be marked as "QUALIFIED — Evidence Quality Limitation." |
| **Chain of custody is broken** | Reconstruct the chain if possible. If not, treat the evidence as unreliable. | Escalate to Evidence Governance. | The evidence item is demoted to "Unreliable — Not for Reliance." Outputs dependent on it are blocked. |
| **Evidence is challenged** | Re-examine the evidence. Document the challenge and the response. | Escalate if the challenge is credible and the evidence was relied upon for a material conclusion. | The finding is placed in "Under Review" status pending resolution. |

## 8. Professional Review Boundary

The professional review boundary defines the line between what the system may produce and what requires human professional judgement:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    SYSTEM SPACE                              │
│  Evidence assembly, classification, indexing                 │
│  Initial mapping to principles and governance                │
│  Consistency and completeness checks                         │
│  Draft finding generation                                    │
│  -> ALL SYSTEM OUTPUTS ARE DRAFT                             │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                PROFESSIONAL REVIEW BOUNDARY                  │
│  <- Human professional judgement enters                      │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                   HUMAN SPACE                                │
│  Assessment of evidence sufficiency and appropriateness      │
│  Resolution of contradictions and ambiguities                │
│  Application of professional scepticism                      │
│  Final conclusion and opinion formation                      │
│  Approval and sign-off                                       │
│  -> ALL FINAL OUTPUTS REQUIRE HUMAN SIGN-OFF                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Inviolable rules of the boundary:**
1. No output may cross the boundary from System Space to Human Space without an evidence chain.
2. No output may cross the boundary from Human Space to Client/Regulator Space without human sign-off.
3. The system may not represent any output as final, approved, or ready for reliance.
4. Human professionals may not delegate their judgement to the system under any circumstance.

## 9. Draft vs Approved Distinction

| Attribute | Draft | Approved |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| **Author** | System (assisted by preparer) | Human professional (assisted by system) |
| **Status indicator** | WATERMARK: "DRAFT — NOT FOR RELIANCE" | WATERMARK: "APPROVED — [Date] — [Approver]" |
| **Evidence chain** | Registered but not yet reviewed for sufficiency | Reviewed and confirmed sufficient and appropriate |
| **Review state** | Unreviewed or under review | All review points cleared |
| **Distribution** | Internal only. Not for client, regulator, or third party. | Per distribution list in engagement terms. |
| **Revision** | May be revised freely within System Space. | Revisions require a new approval cycle. |
| **Reliance** | Not for reliance by any party. | Suitable for reliance within the scope defined in the engagement. |
| **Archival** | Retained for audit trail. | Retained as official record of the engagement. |

**Transition from Draft to Approved requires:**
1. All evidence items registered and chain of custody verified.
2. All review points addressed and cleared.
3. Professional judgement applied and documented.
4. Approver's digital sign-off recorded with timestamp and rationale.
5. Draft watermark replaced with Approved watermark.
