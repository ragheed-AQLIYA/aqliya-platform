# Quality Audit Report — Draft v0.1 Theory Documents

## 1. Overall Quality Rating: **B+**

The Draft v0.1 corpus is exceptionally doctrinally pure, structurally consistent, and domain-specific. The template enforcement (Purpose → Thesis → Problem → Why Existing Systems Fail → Philosophy → Core Principles → 13 implication sections) produces consistent, high-quality output across all 240 drafted documents.

**Strengths:**
- Remarkably consistent doctrinal alignment — no document sampled advocates autonomous AI, dashboard-first patterns, chatbot positioning, or generic SaaS framing
- The five reviewed anchors (01.01, 01.03, 02.01, 04.01, 05.01) provide clear boundaries that prevent category drift
- "AI assists. Humans decide." and "Evidence is the unit of trust." are enforced throughout
- Anti-pattern sections are generally strong and domain-specific

**Gaps:**
- Several documents lack dedicated anti-pattern sections (patterns distributed through "Why Existing Systems Fail" tables)
- Architecture implications are weak in market, commercial, and narrative documents
- Some documents overuse identical doctrinal phrases without domain-specific adaptation
- Commercial implications sections are minimal in most theory documents (acceptable for their type, but worth noting)

## 5. Documents with Duplicated Generic Content

**Minor concern — not systemic.**

The "Why Existing Systems Fail" sections follow a structurally similar pattern across documents. However, each is domain-tailored with different failure modes for different contexts. This is template discipline, not copy-paste.

The recurring doctrinal phrases ("governance is structural, not procedural", "evidence is the unit of trust", "AI assists. Humans decide.") appear in nearly every document. This is intentional doctrinal reinforcement. Some documents could benefit from adapting the phrasing to their specific domain rather than repeating identical language.
