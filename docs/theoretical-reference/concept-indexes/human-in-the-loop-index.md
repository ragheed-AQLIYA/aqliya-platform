# Human-in-the-Loop

## Definition
Human-in-the-Loop means that AI operates within governed boundaries: it recommends, signals, and assists — but every material decision, approval, and conclusion requires attributable human authority.

## Why It Matters
It prevents automation from eroding accountability. In regulated environments, every material outcome must trace to a human decision-maker, and HITL is the architectural mechanism that guarantees this separation.

## Governance Implications
Human decision joints are structurally enforced by the workflow engine. The system may not bypass, queue-skip, or batch-approve material decisions — each one requires an independent human act.

## AuditOS Relevance
Every audit finding and recommendation requires human review before publication. AuditOS embeds HITL at every workflow junction, preventing drafts from becoming outputs without attributable approval.

## Notes for Future Expansion
As AI capabilities advance, the boundary of what constitutes a "material decision" may need periodic recalibration by the governance board.
