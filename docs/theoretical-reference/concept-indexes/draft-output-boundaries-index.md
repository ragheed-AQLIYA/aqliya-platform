# Draft Output Boundaries

## Definition
Draft Output Boundaries define what the system may produce autonomously (drafts) vs. what requires human review (approved outputs). All financial statements, notes, and findings generated by the system are drafts requiring professional review.

## Why It Matters
Without explicit draft boundaries, there is no structural distinction between machine-generated content and professionally approved content. These boundaries prevent accidental or implicit reliance on unapproved AI output in regulated contexts.

## Governance Implications
Draft outputs must be clearly marked. No draft may be presented as final without attributable human approval. The system must enforce visual and data-level segregation between draft and approved states.

## AuditOS Relevance
Financial statements and notes are always draft until reviewer-approved. AuditOS marks all AI-generated content as draft by default, and the workflow engine prevents draft content from appearing in published reports.

## Notes for Future Expansion
As AQLIYA enters new domains, product-specific draft boundary rules may be needed, but the core principle that AI output is draft until reviewed should remain inviolable.
