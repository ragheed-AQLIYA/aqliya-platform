# Professional Judgment

## Definition
Professional Judgment is the exclusive domain of human reviewers. AI assists but never replaces the professional's assessment of evidence, materiality, and conclusions.

## Why It Matters
It preserves the legal and ethical accountability that only a qualified human can bear. In audit, regulatory, and financial contexts, no algorithm may assume the authority of a licensed professional.

## Governance Implications
All professional judgments must be attributable and traceable. The system must log the identity, timestamp, basis, and context of every human override or approval, creating an unbroken chain of accountability.

## AuditOS Relevance
Every recommendation and finding requires human approval before it becomes an accepted output. AuditOS enforces this by design — the workflow engine blocks publication until a named professional has exercised judgment.

## Notes for Future Expansion
As AQLIYA expands into adjacent domains (e.g., tax, compliance), the definition of who qualifies as a "professional" may need domain-specific refinement.
