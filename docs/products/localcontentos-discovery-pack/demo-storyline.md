# LocalContentOS — Demo Storyline

**Status:** Discovery / Planned (not implemented)
**Version:** 1.0 — Discovery Pack
**Format:** Concept / Workflow Demo (no functional UI)
**Duration:** 10–15 minutes

---

## Demo Setup

| Element             | Detail                                                                   |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Demo Type**       | Concept walkthrough — no functional application                          |
| **Format**          | Slide deck + workflow diagram + sample data visuals                      |
| **Target Audience** | Procurement Director, Local Content Manager, CFO                         |
| **Scenario**        | Saudi manufacturing company preparing its quarterly local content report |
| **Data Used**       | Sample anonymized vendor list, spend data, contracts                     |

---

## Demo Script

---

### 1. Opening Pitch (1 min)

**Message:**
"LocalContentOS is a governed local content measurement, evidence, and reporting system built on the AQLIYA platform. It helps organizations like yours move from scattered Excel files and manual reviews to a structured, evidence-backed, auditable local content workflow.

This demo walks through how LocalContentOS would work — from data upload to approved report — using sample data."

**Key point:** This is a concept demonstration. The product is not yet implemented. We are showing the workflow design and value proposition.

---

### 2. The Problem (1.5 min)

**Visual:** Split screen — Excel chaos vs. governed workflow

**Narrative:**
"Today, your local content process probably looks like this:

- Vendor data in one spreadsheet
- Spend data in another
- Contracts in a shared drive
- Evidence scattered across email attachments and paper files
- Classification: manual, inconsistent, no audit trail
- Reporting: someone stays up late compiling it all

The result: you cannot confidently answer 'What is our real local content percentage?' or show an auditor how you arrived at the number."

---

### 3. Upload Dataset (1.5 min)

**Visual:** Import screen mockup — drag-and-drop CSV/XLSX

**Narrative:**
"LocalContentOS starts with data. The procurement team uploads three files:

1. Vendor master list — names, IDs, registration data
2. Procurement spend report — transactions for the period
3. Supplier classification — their current view of local vs. non-local

The system imports, validates, and links the data — flagging duplicates and missing fields automatically."

---

### 4. Supplier Classification (1.5 min)

**Visual:** Vendor list with locality tags — Local (green), Non-Local (red), Mixed (yellow), Undetermined (grey)

**Narrative:**
"The system classifies suppliers based on registration, ownership, and available evidence.

Here you can see:

- Green tags: clearly local suppliers with Saudi CR and ownership
- Red tags: non-local suppliers
- Yellow: mixed — local distributor for a non-local manufacturer
- Grey: undetermined — needs human review

The local content officer reviews each grey tag and either classifies it or requests more evidence."

---

### 5. Spend Review (1.5 min)

**Visual:** Bar chart — Local vs. Non-Local Spend by Category

**Narrative:**
"Once suppliers are classified, the system applies those classifications to every transaction.

The spend review shows:

- Total spend: 50M SAR
- Local spend: 32M SAR (64%)
- Non-local spend: 18M SAR (36%)

Drill down by category reveals which areas have the strongest and weakest local content."

---

### 6. Evidence Gap (1.5 min)

**Visual:** Evidence coverage matrix — green (evidence attached), red (missing), amber (expired)

**Narrative:**
"Not all classifications have supporting evidence. The system flags gaps:

- 3 suppliers classified as 'Local' have no CR uploaded (red)
- 2 local content certificates are expired (red)
- 5 suppliers have only self-declaration without documents (amber)

This is where Excel fails — you can't easily see what evidence is missing and what needs renewal.

The system tells you exactly what you need to fix."

---

### 7. Exception Handling (1.5 min)

**Visual:** Exception list with severity badges — Critical, Warning, Info

**Narrative:**
"Based on evidence gaps and data inconsistencies, the system flags exceptions:

**Critical:** Supplier ABC-123 classified as Local but CR shows non-Saudi registration
→ Requires override with justification and management approval

**Warning:** Spend with supplier XYZ exceeds 30% of total — concentration risk flagged

**Info:** 2 suppliers were reclassified this period compared to last

The local content officer reviews each exception, adds notes, and either clears it or escalates."

---

### 8. Management Review (1 min)

**Visual:** Report summary screen — metrics, findings, exceptions count

**Narrative:**
"The report moves to management review. The procurement director sees:

- Local content metrics
- Key findings and gaps
- Exception summary
- Recommendations

They can add comments, return for revision, or approve.

The key: management sees not just the number, but how the number was calculated, what evidence supports it, and what exceptions exist."

---

### 9. Approval (1 min)

**Visual:** Approval screen — CFO dashboard with Approve / Return buttons, full audit trail

**Narrative:**
"Once approved, the report is locked. No more changes.

The CFO sees:

- Final local content percentage
- Period-over-period trend
- Top gaps to address next quarter
- Full approval chain

Approval is final. The system records who approved, when, and from what data."

---

### 10. Report Export (1 min)

**Visual:** Export package files — PDF report, XLSX data, evidence index

**Narrative:**
"The system generates a complete export package:

1. **PDF report** — Executive summary with metrics and findings (management-ready)
2. **XLSX data** — Detailed transaction-level data with classifications (for analysts)
3. **Evidence index** — List of all evidence files linked to classifications (for auditors)

Ready for tender submissions, board reporting, or internal compliance review."

---

### 11. Closing Question (1 min)

**Message:**
"This is the LocalContentOS concept.

The product is not built yet — and that's exactly why we want to talk to you.

We are looking for a **pilot partner** to work with us on designing and validating this workflow. You provide real data and expertise. We provide the analysis, structure, and a concrete report at the end.

What would local content measurement look like if you had a governed system instead of spreadsheets?

Would you be interested in a pilot?"
