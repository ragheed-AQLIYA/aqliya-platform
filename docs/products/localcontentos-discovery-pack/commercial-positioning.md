## Why Workflow Governance Matters

Local content measurement is not a calculation exercise — it is a governance exercise.

- **Trust:** Stakeholders must trust the local content number. Trust comes from evidence and review, not from a formula.
- **Accountability:** Someone must be responsible for each classification, each exception decision, each override.
- **Auditability:** Regulators, clients, and internal audit must be able to trace how every number was derived.
- **Repeatability:** The process must work the same way each period, producing comparable results.

Local content governance is what LocalContentOS enables — not just a number, but the story behind the number.

## Objections and Answers

| Objection                                            | Answer                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ---------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "We already track local content in Excel."           | Excel has no audit trail, no evidence linking, no workflow governance. You have numbers — but can you prove them?                                                                                   |
| "Our ERP can do this."                               | ERPs handle procurement transactions. They don't manage local content evidence, review workflows, exception handling, or audit trails. This is a different layer.                                   |
| "This is just another system to learn."              | LocalContentOS works with data from your existing systems — it doesn't replace them. It adds governance above your ERP and spreadsheets.                                                            |
| "We don't have a regulatory requirement yet."        | The market is moving toward more formal local content requirements. Preparing now gives you a head start — and a governed history when requirements arrive.                                         |
| "How is this different from AuditOS?"                | AuditOS is for financial audit and assurance. LocalContentOS is for local content measurement and governance. Different data, different users, different workflows. Both share the AQLIYA platform. |
| "Do you integrate with LCGPA?"                       | Not currently. LocalContentOS is an internal governance system that helps you prepare evidence-backed reports. Direct regulatory integration is a future possibility, not a current feature.        |
| "The product isn't built yet — why should I engage?" | That's exactly the opportunity. You can help shape the product to fit real Saudi market needs. Early pilot partners get preferential pricing and influence on the product direction.                |
