# LocalContentOS — Objection Handling

**Status:** Sales pack — product not implemented as software
**Version:** 1.0

---

## Objection 1: "Is this approved by LCGPA?"

**Answer:**
LocalContentOS is an internal governance and evidence management system. It is not a regulatory submission platform and does not require LCGPA approval. Think of it as a tool to prepare evidence-backed reports — not as a submission portal.

**Follow-up:**
"LCGPA sets the standards. LocalContentOS helps you organize the evidence to meet those standards. The two are complementary."

---

## Objection 2: "Can this submit reports officially?"

**Answer:**
No. The pilot produces draft management materials, not official submissions. The outputs are designed to help you prepare for regulatory reporting — but the submission itself remains your responsibility through existing channels.

**Follow-up:**
"Direct regulatory integration is a future possibility once the product is built. For now, the value is in having governed, evidence-backed data ready for whatever submission format is required."

---

## Objection 3: "We already use Excel."

**Answer:**
Excel is great for many things. Local content governance is not one of them. In Excel:

- Anyone can change a number with no audit trail
- Evidence is stored separately or not at all
- Versions multiply and nobody knows which is current
- Reviews happen by email with no formal approval

The pilot shows you what governed local content looks like — without forcing you to stop using Excel today.

---

## Objection 4: "We already have ERP / procurement reports."

**Answer:**
ERPs are excellent at procurement transactions. They are not designed for:

- Supplier locality classification with evidence
- Local content calculation with traceability
- Evidence vault with confidence levels
- Review and approval workflow
- Exception tracking and management reporting

LocalContentOS sits above your ERP, adding the governance layer that ERPs lack for local content.

---

## Objection 5: "Can AI calculate this automatically?"

**Answer:**
AI can assist with classification suggestions, but local content is a governance exercise — not just a calculation. The AQLIYA trust principle applies: AI assists, humans decide, evidence governs. An AI-only approach cannot provide the audit trail and human accountability that local content requires.

**Follow-up:**
"When the product is built, AI will assist with classification. But even then, every decision will be human-reviewed and evidence-linked."

---

## Objection 6: "Is the formula official?"

**Answer:**
The formulas used in the pilot are conceptual — they follow common local content logic (local spend / total spend). They are not official regulatory formulas. Any formula must be validated against official requirements and your specific local content policy before production use.

**Follow-up:**
"The pilot uses transparent, documented logic. If you have specific regulatory formulas or customer-specific criteria, we can incorporate them."

---

## Objection 7: "Our data is sensitive."

**Answer:**
We understand. We offer several options:

- Data anonymization (rounded amounts, aggregated categories)
- NDA or data processing agreement
- Customer-defined sensitivity classification per data type
- Option to redact specific sensitive fields while keeping classification data

The pilot never shares your data outside the engagement.

---

## Objection 8: "We are not ready for software."

**Answer:**
That is exactly why the pilot exists. There is no software to install, no system to learn, no integration project. It is a service engagement — an analyst works with your data using templates.

When you are ready for software, LocalContentOS can be built on the AQLIYA platform. The pilot helps you decide if it is worth the investment.

---

## Objection 9: "Why not wait until the platform is built?"

**Answer:**
Waiting means:

- You continue with the same ungoverned process today
- You miss the opportunity to shape the product to your needs
- You start from zero when you eventually need a governed approach

Pilot partners get preferential pricing, influence on product direction, and a head start on their local content governance journey.
