# LocalContentOS — Pilot-to-Paid Conversion Plan

**Status:** Sales pack — product not implemented as software
**Version:** 1.0

---

## Conversion Philosophy

The pilot is designed to convert, not to sell. If the pilot delivers genuine value, conversion is a natural outcome. If it does not, conversion would be premature regardless of sales pressure.

**Key principle:** Let the pilot results speak. Never oversell.

---

## Conversion Moments During Pilot

| Moment                | Signal                                                      | Action                                         |
| --------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| **Kickoff**           | Customer is engaged, asks good questions, commits resources | Establish value perception early               |
| **Data intake**       | Customer provides clean, complete data quickly              | Reinforce efficiency of structured approach    |
| **First findings**    | Customer reacts with "we didn't know this"                  | Highlight new visibility into their data       |
| **Evidence gaps**     | Customer realizes how much evidence is missing              | Position as risk identification, not criticism |
| **Management review** | Management engages with findings, makes decisions           | Demonstrate value of governed process          |
| **Decision meeting**  | Customer asks "what next?" unprompted                       | Present options and recommendation             |

---

## Decision Criteria

At the end of the pilot, assess:

| Criterion           | Check                                  | Green                                    | Yellow                    | Red                  |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------- | -------------------- |
| Data quality        | Customer provided clean data           | ✅ All required files complete and clean | ⚠️ Minor gaps, resolvable | ❌ Major data issues |
| Engagement          | Customer SME was responsive            | ✅ Weekly check-ins productive           | ⚠️ Some delays            | ❌ Unresponsive      |
| Management buy-in   | Sponsor/decision maker participated    | ✅ Attended findings meeting             | ⚠️ Absent but briefed     | ❌ No engagement     |
| Findings acceptance | Customer agreed with findings          | ✅ Accepted with minor clarification     | ⚠️ Disputed some findings | ❌ Rejected findings |
| Budget readiness    | Customer has budget for next step      | ✅ Budget identified                     | ⚠️ Budget process needed  | ❌ No budget         |
| Timeline urgency    | Customer has a deadline driving action | ✅ Urgent need (tender, audit)           | ⚠️ Nice-to-have           | ❌ No timeline       |

**Conversion readiness:** 5–6 Green → Ready. 3–4 Green → Conditional. < 3 Green → Not ready.

## Paid Options After Pilot

| Element             | Detail                                                             |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **When to propose** | Customer has clear need, budget, and timeline. Pilot score > 2.5.  |
| **What it is**      | Build LocalContentOS as a working workspace on AQLIYA Core         |

| Element             | Detail                                                     |

## Extension Logic

| Scenario                                       | Action                                                            |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Customer engaged but needs more data           | Extend pilot 2–4 weeks, no additional cost if scope is reasonable |
| Customer wants to try another category         | Propose as paid extension (50% of original pilot fee)             |
| Customer wants full enterprise scope           | Transition directly to MVP proposal                               |
| Customer is interested but budget not approved | Offer 90-day payment terms or milestone-based invoicing           |
| Customer needs board approval                  | Provide executive summary deck for internal presentation          |

---

## When to Propose Software MVP

Propose the software MVP when:

- ✅ Pilot success score ≥ 2.5
- ✅ Evidence governance demonstrated value to the customer
- ✅ Customer management engaged and made decisions based on pilot findings
- ✅ Customer has budget allocated or committed
- ✅ Customer has a time-sensitive driver (tender, audit, regulatory deadline)
- ✅ Customer explicitly asks "can we have this as a system?"

---

## When to Propose Recurring Analyst-Led Review

Propose recurring review when:

- ✅ Customer values the analysis but is not ready for software investment
- ✅ Customer needs regular local content monitoring (quarterly/annual)
- ✅ Customer has multiple periods to track
- ✅ Customer's procurement data changes significantly between periods
- ✅ Customer wants to build an evidence base before committing to software

---

## When to Pause

Pause the commercial process when:

- ❌ Customer does not have a decision maker identified
- ❌ Pilot score < 2.0 — critical gaps remain
- ❌ Customer disputed findings without reasonable basis
- ❌ Customer has no budget timeline
- ❌ Internal champion left or changed roles

**Pause action:** Archive pilot files. Set a 6-month revisit date. Send a quarterly check-in email.
