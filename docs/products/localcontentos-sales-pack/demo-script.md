## Demo Narrative Using Templates

When walking through the templates, say:

> "These templates are not a system. They are the structured input format. An analyst imports them into a working environment (Excel/Google Sheets), applies classification rules, links evidence, and tracks exceptions. The magic is not the tool — it is the governed process."

---

## How to Explain Evidence Review

Use a concrete example:

> "Vendor A is classified as 'Local' with 1.2M SAR spend. The evidence attached is a self-declaration — confidence: Low. Vendor B is classified as 'Local' with a valid CR and LCGPA certificate — confidence: High.
>
> Both show as 'Local' in the numbers. But only Vendor B's classification is fully defensible. The pilot shows you this gap so you can fix it."

---

## How to Explain Findings

> "A finding is not a criticism. It is a structured observation: here is what we expected, here is what we found, here is the evidence gap, here is the financial impact, here is our recommendation. Management then decides what to do."

---

## How to Explain Pilot Outcome

> "The pilot outcome is a decision memo that says: your local content is X%, but only Y% is backed by High-confidence evidence. Here are the gaps you need to close. Here is our recommendation for what to do next. The decision is yours."
