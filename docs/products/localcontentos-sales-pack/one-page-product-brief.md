## The Problem

Organizations in Saudi Arabia struggle to measure and report local content reliably.

| Challenge                                           | Impact                               |
| --------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Data scattered across Excel files, ERPs, and emails | No single source of truth            |
| Classification is manual and inconsistent           | Numbers cannot be trusted or audited |
| Evidence is missing or expired                      | Claims are not defensible            |
| No governed workflow                                | Nobody owns the process              |

---

## Why It Matters

Local content is a strategic priority under Saudi Vision 2030. Organizations face:

- Increasing local content requirements from government and semi-government clients
- Internal compliance and reporting obligations
- Tender submissions requiring evidence-backed local content declarations
- Audit readiness for local content claims

---

## What LocalContentOS Does

1. **Structured data collection** — templates for vendor master, procurement spend, contracts, evidence
2. **Supplier and spend classification** — local vs. non-local based on evidence, not guesswork
3. **Evidence management** — link every classification to verifiable documentation
4. **Exception handling** — systematic identification and review of gaps
5. **Management reporting** — clear findings, recommendations, and decision-ready summaries

---

## What It Does NOT Do

- It is NOT a regulatory submission system
- It does NOT integrate with LCGPA
- It is NOT an AI-only automatic classifier
- It does NOT replace your ERP or procurement system
- It does NOT provide binding legal or compliance determinations
- It is NOT implemented as software yet
