# Pilot Success Criteria — LocalContentOS v0.1

## Definition of Pilot Success

The pilot is successful when the customer can demonstrate understanding of the governed assessment workflow and confirm the platform's value for their local content measurement needs. All criteria below must pass for a "successful pilot" closeout.

## Measurable Criteria

| #   | Criterion                        | How to Measure                                                     | Minimum Bar                                    |
| --- | -------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------ | ---------------------------------------------- |
| 1   | Supplier data imported           | Customer's supplier list visible in the supplier register          | At least 10 suppliers loaded and displayed     |
| 2   | Spend records loaded             | Spend records visible with amounts, categories, and supplier links | At least 20 records loaded                     |
| 3   | Supplier classification reviewed | Each supplier has a locality badge (محلي/غير محلي/مشترك)           | All suppliers classified                       |
| 4   | Evidence coverage reviewed       | Evidence items visible with status badges                          | At least 5 evidence items linked to records    |
| 5   | Findings identified              | At least one finding visible in the findings register              | Customer acknowledges at least one gap or risk |
| 6   | Review submitted                 | Review record created with reviewer identity and timestamp         | One review submitted                           |
| 7   | Approval decision recorded       | Approval or rejection recorded with identity and timestamp         | One decision recorded                          |
| 8   | Report generated                 | Report record created with disclaimer                              | One report generated                           |
| 9   | Audit trail verified             | Customer can see the event log                                     | Customer confirms they can trace actions       |
| 10  | Customer can explain value       | Customer describes how the platform differs from spreadsheets      | Verbal or written confirmation                 |

## Qualitative Criteria

| #   | Criterion                       | Indicator                                                                                |
| --- | ------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| 11  | Customer engaged                | Customer stakeholders attend scheduled sessions, respond to requests                     |
| 12  | Data provided on time           | Customer delivers data within the agreed timeline                                        |
| 13  | No misunderstanding about scope | Customer does not expect regulatory certification, AI autonomy, or production deployment |
| 14  | Customer sees next step         | Customer can articulate what a paid engagement would look like                           |

## What Success Does NOT Mean

- Customer signs a contract (that is conversion, not pilot success)
- All 13 mutation-only QA items are tested (desirable but not blocking)
- Regulator-certified PDF/XLSX or L6 export hardening (binary export exists; Arabic font P2; not official compliance)
- Full ERP integration is live (excluded from scope)
