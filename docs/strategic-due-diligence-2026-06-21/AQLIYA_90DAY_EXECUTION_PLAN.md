## Phase 1 — 90-Day Objectives

### Primary Objective

**By September 22, 2026: One signed pilot agreement with a paying Saudi institutional customer.**

"Signed" means: both parties have executed the agreement. "Paying" means: a commercial commitment exists — minimum pilot fee, retainer, or in-kind commitment with conversion clause. "Saudi institutional" means: a real organization with a real compliance, audit, or local content obligation.

### Secondary Objective

**By September 22, 2026: Commercial Lead hired (offer accepted and start date confirmed) AND live LLM operational in staging.**

These two are non-negotiable preconditions for Q4 2026 pilot delivery. If either is missing on Day 90, the Q4 plan collapses.

### Discovery Interview Framework

**Pre-Call Preparation (10 minutes before every call):**
- Research the organization: recent regulatory news, recent tenders, IKTVA score (if public), recent audit findings
- Know their sector's regulatory environment
- Prepare 3 hypotheses about their pain based on research

**Opening (5 minutes):**
> "I'm the founder of AQLIYA. We're building a governed intelligence platform for Saudi institutions dealing with audit and local content compliance. Before I tell you anything about what we've built, I want to understand your world. I have 6 questions. Can we spend 30 minutes on those?"

**Core Questions (25 minutes):**

1. **Current State:**
   - "Walk me through how your organization manages [audit trails / local content reporting] today. What tools? Who is involved? How long does it take?"

2. **Pain Points:**
   - "Where in that process do things break down? What keeps you or your team up at night about compliance?"
   - "When was the last time you had a compliance issue or audit finding that you wish you'd caught earlier?"

3. **Consequence:**
   - "What happens if you miss an IKTVA reporting deadline? What is the actual business impact of a compliance gap?"
   - "Who in your organization feels that pain most acutely?"

4. **Current Solutions:**
   - "Are you using any software for this? What does it do well? Where does it fall short?"
   - "Have you evaluated other solutions in the last 12 months?"

5. **Decision Process:**
   - "If you found a solution that solved this problem completely, how would the purchase decision work? Who would need to be involved?"
   - "What would need to be true for you to sign something in the next 60–90 days?"

6. **Future State:**
   - "If this problem were completely solved — what would your team's time look like? What would you be doing differently?"
   - "What does good look like 12 months from now?"

**Closing (5 minutes):**
> "This is incredibly helpful. Based on what you've described, we may have something that could help. Can I send you a one-page overview and we can have a follow-up conversation in 2 weeks? I'm not going to pitch you — I want to show you something specific and see if it resonates."

**What NOT to say on discovery calls:**
- "Our platform has X features" — not yet
- "We're the only platform that does Y" — not yet
- "Our pricing is Z" — not yet
- Anything that sounds like a pitch

## Phase 7 — Commercial Lead Hiring Plan

### Why This Is The Most Important Decision In The Next 90 Days

The right commercial lead shortens the time to first revenue by 3–6 months and improves deal quality by providing existing relationships and enterprise sales experience. The wrong commercial lead delays first revenue by 6–12 months and consumes significant founder time in recovery. This decision is worth 2 weeks of intensive focus.

### Candidate Profile — Non-Negotiable Requirements

| Requirement | Description | How to Verify |
|-------------|-------------|--------------|
| **Arabic-first** | Primary business language is Arabic. Presents, negotiates, and closes in Arabic. | Ask them to present 5 minutes of the sales deck in Arabic in the final interview |
| **Saudi institutional network** | Has 10+ relationships at C-level or VP-level at Saudi companies in energy, financial services, manufacturing, or government-adjacent sectors | Ask them to name 10 specific contacts they could call today |
| **Enterprise sales close record** | Has personally closed at least 3 enterprise software deals (>100,000 SAR each), not assisted | Verify with reference calls to the buyers, not their managers |
| **Compliance/governance adjacent** | Has sold governance, compliance, audit, ERP, regulatory, or risk software — OR has deep domain knowledge in one of the above | Cannot be taught; must be pre-existing |
| **Startup tolerance** | Has worked in a pre-Series A environment, OR explicitly understands no sales support infrastructure exists yet | Assess through behavioral questions about past low-resource environments |

### Preferred Background

- Former employee of a Big 4 firm's technology or advisory arm in Saudi Arabia, who moved to enterprise software sales
- Former enterprise software sales at SAP, Oracle, Thomson Reuters, Wolters Kluwer in Saudi Arabia
- Former VP of compliance or internal audit at a Saudi institution, who crossed over to consulting/advisory/technology sales
- Saudi national with Gulf-wide enterprise software sales experience

### Red Flags (Disqualifiers)

| Red Flag | Why It Disqualifies |
|----------|-------------------|
| Has only sold horizontal software (CRM, productivity, project management) | Cannot speak to governance, compliance, or audit buyers in their language |
| Expects a large inbound marketing team to generate leads | No such team exists and won't for 12 months |
| Has never closed a deal >100,000 SAR | AQLIYA's minimum deal size is above this threshold |
| Cannot name 10 specific contacts without thinking for >2 minutes | Their network is not as relevant as claimed |
| Expects to "follow up" on leads rather than develop them | AQLIYA has no warm inbound leads; must create them |
| First question is about commission rate, not about the product | Signals transactional not strategic orientation |
| No Arabic business communication ability | Non-negotiable |

### Interview Process

**Round 1: 30-minute screening (phone/video)**
- Tell me about the largest enterprise software deal you personally closed. Walk me through the full process from first meeting to signature.
- Who is the most senior Saudi institutional buyer you have closed a deal with in the last 3 years? Can I speak with them as a reference?
- Name 10 people in your network who could be a potential customer for a governance AI platform used by Saudi audit and compliance teams.
- What do you know about IKTVA, NIDLP, or SOCPA requirements?

**Round 2: 90-minute deep dive (in person preferred)**
- Present the AQLIYA Sales Deck v1 back to the founder in Arabic, as if to a CFO. (They receive the deck 24 hours before.)
- Role-play a discovery call with the founder playing a skeptical CCO.
- Walk through your 30-day plan: who would you call, in what order, with what message?
- What is your compensation expectation and how do you think about equity?

**Round 3: Reference calls (2 required)**
- Call the buyer they closed the deal from Round 1, not their manager.
- Call a peer from their last company who worked alongside them.

Questions for references:
- Did they close the deal themselves, or were they part of a team that closed it?
- What did they do when a deal stalled for 3 months?
- Would you hire them again? Why or why not?

### Hiring Scorecard

| Dimension | Weight | Score 1–5 | Weighted Score |
|-----------|--------|-----------|----------------|
| Arabic-first communication | 20% | /5 | /1.0 |
| Saudi institutional network depth | 25% | /5 | /1.25 |
| Enterprise software close record | 20% | /5 | /1.0 |
| Compliance/governance domain knowledge | 15% | /5 | /0.75 |
| Startup environment fit | 10% | /5 | /0.5 |
| Compensation/equity expectation alignment | 10% | /5 | /0.5 |

**Decision threshold:**
- Score ≥ 4.0: Strong hire. Proceed.
- Score 3.5–3.9: Conditional hire. Proceed with clear 30-day performance expectations.
- Score < 3.5: Do not hire. Continue search.

### Compensation Structure

**Option A: Co-Founder**
- Equity: 8–15% (4-year vest, 1-year cliff)
- Salary: Below-market (70–80% of senior sales market rate in Saudi Arabia)
- Commission: 5–8% of first-year contract value for deals they close

**Option B: Commercial Director**
- Equity: 3–5% (4-year vest, 1-year cliff)
- Salary: Market rate for senior enterprise sales in Riyadh (estimated 25,000–40,000 SAR/month depending on experience)
- Commission: 8–12% of first-year contract value for deals they close

**Option C: VP Sales (later stage)**

### Enforcement Rule

Every technical task must pass this test before work begins:

> "Does this task either: (A) unblock a customer conversation happening in the next 14 days, OR (B) fix a confirmed security vulnerability?"

If the answer is NO to both: the task goes to the deferred backlog.  
If the founder finds themselves working on something that fails this test, they must stop and redirect to a commercial activity.
