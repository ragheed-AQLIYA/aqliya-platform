# AQLIYA Competitive Positioning

**Classification:** Board GTM / Product Strategy  
**Date:** 2026-06-19  
**Scope:** Enterprise institutional software competitive landscape

---

## Positioning Statement

**AQLIYA wins as the governed, Arabic-first institutional intelligence layer for evidence-heavy workflows in Saudi Arabia—not as a general AI assistant or ERP replacement.**

---

## Competitive Matrix Summary

| Competitor | Category | AQLIYA wins | AQLIYA loses |
|------------|----------|-------------|--------------|
| **Microsoft Copilot** | Horizontal AI assist | Governance, evidence, audit trail, domain workflows (LC, audit) | Ubiquity, M365 integration, brand, price bundling |
| **SAP Joule** | ERP-embedded AI | Agility, LC-specific workflow, Arabic UX, faster pilot | ERP entrenchment, procurement relationships |
| **ServiceNow** | Enterprise workflow | Domain depth (audit/LC), regional focus, price | ITSM scale, global SI ecosystem |
| **AuditBoard** | Audit management | Arabic, TB factory, local AI option, regional pricing | US audit market share, SOC2 maturity, references |
| **TeamMate (Wolters Kluwer)** | Audit methodology | AI assist + governance narrative, modern UX | 30-year audit firm entrenchment, methodology certification |
| **Local Saudi competitors** | LC consultants / spreadsheets | Productized workflow, evidence graph, ERP import | Relationships, on-ground services, Arabic consulting trust |

## Detailed Comparisons

### vs Microsoft Copilot

| Dimension | Copilot | AQLIYA |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| **Value prop** | Productivity in Office | Governed institutional outcomes |
| **Evidence chain** | Weak | Core design |
| **Human review gates** | Optional | Required before export |
| **Audit trail** | Limited | Platform-native |
| **Arabic institutional UX** | Generic | Arabic-first |
| **Domain (LC, audit)** | None | Deep |

**Win message:** "Copilot helps you write faster. AQLIYA helps your institution **prove** what was decided, by whom, with what evidence."

**Lose scenario:** Buyer wants email/Excel AI only, already all-in on M365 E5.

**Strategy:** Don't compete in Office productivity. Compete in **governed workflow + export**.

---

### vs SAP Joule

| Dimension | Joule | AQLIYA |
|-----------|-------|--------|
| **Integration** | Native SAP | ERP connectors (SAP/Oracle/CSV) |
| **LC workflow** | Generic analytics | Purpose-built workbook + review |
| **Time to pilot** | Long (SAP cycle) | Weeks |
| **Governance story** | ERP audit logs | Cross-product evidence graph |

**Win message:** "Joule answers SAP questions. AQLIYA runs your **local content governance program** with evidence and approval."

**Lose scenario:** CIO mandate: "SAP only for all analytics."

**Strategy:** Position as **LC intelligence layer** that imports SAP data—not ERP replacement.

---

### vs ServiceNow

| Dimension | ServiceNow | AQLIYA |
|-----------|------------|--------|
| **Scope** | IT/ESM platform | Institutional domain OS |
| **Customization** | Heavy SI implementation | Pre-built LC/Audit workflows |
| **Regional** | Global | Saudi-first |
| **Price** | Enterprise $$$ | Mid-market pilot pricing |

**Win message:** "ServiceNow is your IT ticket system. AQLIYA is your **audit and local content operating system**."

**Lose scenario:** Group-wide ServiceNow mandate including GRC module.

**Strategy:** Avoid ITSM deals. Target **business sponsors** (CFO, LC manager, audit partner).

---

### vs AuditBoard

| Dimension | AuditBoard | AQLIYA |
|-----------|------------|--------|
| **Market** | US SOX/internal audit | KSA audit + Arabic FS |
| **Maturity** | Public company references | Pilot-stage |
| **AI** | Adding features | Governance-native from day one |
| **TB → FS factory** | Not core | **87% benchmark, Shalfa proof** |

**Win message:** "AuditBoard is built for US public companies. AQLIYA is built for **Arabic audit engagements with AI-assisted TB mapping under human review**."

**Lose scenario:** Big 4 firm standardized globally on AuditBoard.

**Strategy:** Target **regional firms** and **in-house audit** teams; pilot-audited profile as proof.

---

### vs TeamMate (Wolters Kluwer)

| Dimension | TeamMate | AQLIYA |
|-----------|----------|--------|
| **Heritage** | Audit methodology standard | Modern web platform |
| **AI** | Limited | Assistive with review gates |
| **Arabic** | Weak | Strong |
| **Implementation** | Familiar to partners | New entrant |

**Win message:** "TeamMate manages audit files. AQLIYA **accelerates TB classification and FS generation** with governed AI assist."

**Lose scenario:** Partner firm won't change methodology tooling mid-engagement.

**Strategy:** Sell **augmentation** (TB factory, AI mapping) not full methodology replacement in Year 1.

### vs Local Saudi Competitors

| Type | Typical offer | AQLIYA advantage |
|------|---------------|------------------|
| LC consulting firms | Spreadsheets + PDF reports | Repeatable product, audit trail, ERP import |
| Big 4 advisory | High-touch, high cost | Lower cost pilot, software reusability |
| ERP local partners | SAP/Oracle modules | Purpose-built LC workflow + evidence |
| Generic SaaS | English-first CRM/tools | Arabic institutional UX + governance |

**Win message:** "Consultants leave when the project ends. AQLIYA **institutionalizes** your local content program."

**Lose scenario:** Buyer prefers trusted advisor relationship over software; wants certified LCGPA sign-off from consultant.

**Strategy:** Partner with consultants as **implementation channel**—software + their advisory, not replacement.

## Where AQLIYA Wins (Defensible)

1. **Governed AI in high-stakes workflows** — review before export; audit every AI action
2. **Arabic-first institutional UX** — RTL, bilingual exports, Saudi terminology
3. **Local Content Intelligence** — workbook, evidence, ERP, pilot readiness (not certification)
4. **Audit TB → FS factory** — measurable accuracy; Shalfa-class pilot proof
5. **Multi-workflow platform inheritance** — one auth/governance layer for LC + audit + decisions
6. **Speed to pilot** — weeks vs SAP/ServiceNow months
7. **Regional pricing and founder access** — enterprise vendors can't match for mid-market KSA

---

## Where AQLIYA Loses (Honest)

1. **Brand and references** — zero public logos vs incumbents
2. **Enterprise InfoSec** — no SOC2, no pen test report
3. **Ecosystem** — no SI army, no AppStore, no M365 bundle
4. **Global audit methodology certification** — TeamMate/AuditBoard entrenched
5. **ERP replacement narrative** — SAP wins "single system of record"
6. **Horizontal AI** — Copilot "good enough" for drafts
7. **Government RFP scale** — staging/DR/compliance gaps
