## Executive Answer

AQLIYA is a **Private Governed Institutional Intelligence Platform** selling **governed operating systems** to institutions that cannot afford autonomous AI or ungoverned SaaS for high-stakes workflows. The company is real software with real products—not a demo shell—but it is **pilot-capable, not enterprise-commercial yet**.

---

## 1. What business is AQLIYA actually in?

**Answer:** Governed institutional workflow software with assistive AI—not generic SaaS, not a chatbot, not a CRM.

AQLIYA builds **Specialized Operating Systems** (AuditOS, LocalContentOS, DecisionOS) on a shared Intelligence Core. Revenue comes from **annual platform + product subscriptions** and **pilot-to-production conversion**, not from AI tokens or seat-only CRM pricing.

The honest commercial motion today is:

| Motion | What we sell | Who pays |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| **Primary** | Local Content Intelligence (LocalContentOS) | Saudi enterprises with LC obligations |
| **Secondary** | Governed audit workspace (AuditOS) | Audit firms and large finance teams |
| **Tertiary** | Custom governed workflows (WorkflowOS) | Existing client relationships (Sunbul lineage) |

Everything else (SalesOS, RiskOS branding, SimulationOS, Studio) is **internal capability or future optionality**, not a business line.

---

## 2. What problem does it truly solve?

**Answer:** Institutions must make **evidence-backed, reviewable, auditable decisions** in regulated or high-stakes domains—and they cannot trust black-box AI or disconnected spreadsheets.

Concrete problems solved today:

| Problem | Product | Proof |
|---------|---------|-------|
| Audit engagement lifecycle with evidence and export | AuditOS | L5, Shalfa pilot runbook, factory accuracy ≥85% |
| Local content classification with evidence and review | LocalContentOS | L5 conditional, 100% internal pilot readiness (2026-06-17) |
| Committee decision governance with evidence | DecisionOS | L5, bilingual PDF export, 42 action tests |
| Custom institutional workflows with SLA and export gates | WorkflowOS | L5, 31 action tests |

What AQLIYA does **not** solve today: regulatory certification, licensed audit opinions, autonomous compliance decisions, or enterprise procurement at Fortune-500 scale without operator setup.

## 3. Platform or products company?

**Answer:** **Platform company with 2 commercial products and 1 proof product.**

| Layer | Role | Commercial weight |
|-------|------|-------------------|
| **AQLIYA Platform + Intelligence Core** | Shared auth, governance, AI orchestration, audit logs | Enabler—not sold standalone |
| **AuditOS** | Proof product; first customer lineage | Sell now (controlled pilot) |
| **LocalContentOS** | Strategic growth product; Saudi market | **Sell first** |
| **DecisionOS, WorkflowOS, Office AI, LocalContactOS** | Adjacent / shared apps | Upsell after core land |
| **SalesOS** | Internal CRM-lite | **Do not sell** |

The platform narrative is **strategically correct** but **commercially premature** as a standalone pitch. Buyers purchase **a governed outcome** (audit workspace, LC intelligence), not "a platform."

**Board decision:** Lead with **product beachheads**; platform story follows after 2–3 reference customers.

## 4. Unique market position

**Answer:** The only Arabic-first, governance-native institutional intelligence stack that combines **evidence graphs, human review gates, audit trails, and assistive AI** in one modular monolith—positioned for **Saudi/regional institutional buyers** who need private, reviewable systems—not Copilot in Excel.

Differentiators that are **real in code**:

1. **Trust architecture:** Deterministic AI default; human review before export; audit events on mutations (`src/lib/ai/`, governance tests).
2. **Bilingual institutional UX:** Arabic-first RTL across core workflows (next-intl, product seeds).
3. **Domain depth:** Audit factory (TB → FS), LC workbook scoring, decision evidence—not generic workflow templates.
4. **Regional fit:** LocalContentOS aligned to Saudi LC compliance preparation (explicitly not LCGPA certification—see product doctrine).

Differentiators that are **aspirational only**:

- On-Prem / Air-Gap packages (L0)
- Enterprise SSO at scale without operator setup
- SOC2 / ISO certification
- "Platform replaces ERP" narrative

## 5. What is the moat?

**Answer:** **Governance + evidence + domain workflow depth + regional trust**—not AI model quality alone.

| Moat layer | Strength | Durability |
|------------|----------|------------|
| **Workflow + evidence linkage** | High | Hard to replicate without multi-year domain build |
| **Audit trail + review gates** | High | Becomes sticky after first pilot |
| **Arabic institutional UX** | Medium-High | Underserved vs global vendors |
| **Local Content domain (LCOS)** | High | Saudi-specific; ERP connectors + workbook |
| **Audit factory (AuditOS)** | High | Shalfa lineage; TB classification 87% AI benchmark |
| **AI model / LLM** | Low | Commodity; Ollama/cloud interchangeable |
| **Platform breadth (7+ products)** | Negative moat | Sprawl dilutes focus; competitors win on depth |

**Moat killer:** Continuing to build parallel products (SalesOS expansion, RiskOS brand, Studio) instead of **2 reference customers** on AuditOS + LocalContentOS.

## Strategic Implications

1. **Identity is clear and defensible**—do not rebrand as "AI company" or "AuditOS only."
2. **Commercial identity is muddy**—too many L5 labels, too few paying customers documented.
3. **Moat compounds with customer evidence**, not with more product surfaces.
4. **Next 12 months:** Win Saudi LC + one audit reference; freeze everything else.
