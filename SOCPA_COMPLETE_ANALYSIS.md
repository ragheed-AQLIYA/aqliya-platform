### 1.3 Core Responsibilities

1. **Regulation** — Regulation of the accounting and auditing profession in Saudi Arabia
2. **Standard-setting** — Development and adoption of accounting, auditing, and quality standards
3. **Licensing** — Licensing of accountants, auditors, tax accountants, VAT practitioners, and financial consultants
4. **Quality control** — Monitoring professional performance quality (مراقبة جودة الأداء المهني)
5. **Examination** — Professional examinations for fellowship, accounting technician, VAT specialist, IFRS, forensic fraud examiner, readiness test
6. **Membership** — 5 tiers: associate, professional, full (practicing/non-practicing), student, honorary
7. **Training** — Continuing professional education, training courses, Jahiziyah readiness program
8. **Enforcement** — Professional conduct, ethics, anti-money laundering compliance, sanctions
9. **International representation** — Liaison with IASB, IFAC, international bodies
10. **Local content** — Local content verification program aligned with Saudi Vision 2030

### 3.3 Quality Control & Inspection Workflow

**Current Workflow:**
1. SOCPA identifies practitioners for quality review (4 categories: CPAs, tax, VAT, accounting)
2. Practitioner submits documentation
3. Peer reviewer or SOCPA reviewer examines working papers
4. Findings documented
5. Practitioner responds
6. SOCPA issues quality rating
7. Remediation if needed
8. Possible sanctions

**Pain Points:**
- Manual document submission (likely paper/PDF-based)
- No standardized digital working paper format
- Reviewer selection and assignment manual
- Finding tracking lacks automation
- Remediation progress not digitized
- No real-time quality monitoring
